Principles of Operation

The instrument cluster, and the restraints control module (RCM) control the following chimes:

  • safety belt warning (repetitive chime)
  • key-in-ignition warning (repetitive chime)
  • headlamps on warning (repetitive chime)
  • door ajar warning (single tone)
  • air bag warning (repetitive chime)

The following 5 distinct chime rates are used to indicate warning:

  • safety belt warning is 1 chime per second
  • key-in-ignition warning is 2 chimes per second
  • headlamps on warning is 4 chimes per second
  • door ajar warning is a single chime
  • air bag warning is 5 chimes per second

Safety Belt Warning Chime

The safety belt warning chime warns the driver safety belt is not fastened. The safety belt warning chime sounds when the driver safety belt is not fastened and when the ignition lock cylinder is turned from the OFF/LOCK or ACC to the ON or START position. The safety belt warning chime stops sounding if any of the following conditions is met:

  • the safety belt is fastened.
  • the ignition lock cylinder is switched from the ON or START to the OFF/LOCK or ACC position.
  • the chime has sounded for 6 seconds.

The safety belt warning chime inputs are:

  • ignition switch position.
  • driver safety belt switch.

Safety Belt Minder

NOTE: The safety belt minder feature is configurable. To configure using the diagnostic tool, refer to Section 418-01 . To configure without using the diagnostic tool, refer to Safety Belt Minder Deactivating/Activating in this section.

The safety belt minder feature supplements the current safety belt warning function. The safety belt minder feature is enabled after the current safety belt warning is complete. If the driver safety belt is not buckled approximately 5 seconds after the safety belt warning indicator has turned off, the safety belt minder reminds the driver that his/her safety belt is unbuckled by intermittently sounding a chime and illuminating the safety belt warning lamp in the instrument cluster. While activated, the safety belt minder alternates the chime and indicator from on for 6 seconds to off for 30 seconds.

The belt minder reminder stops if any of the following conditions is met:

  • the driver safety belt is buckled.
  • the ignition lock cylinder is turned to the OFF or ACC position.
  • 5 minutes have elapsed since the belt minder has started.

Door Ajar Warning Chime

The door ajar warning chime warns that a door has become ajar. The door ajar warning chime is a single tone with the duration of 1 second. The tone sounds when the driver or passenger door initially becomes ajar while the ignition lock cylinder is in the ON position.

The door ajar warning chime inputs are:

  • ignition switch position.
  • door ajar switches.

Key-In-Ignition Warning Chime

The key-in-ignition warning chime warns that the key is in the ignition lock cylinder when the driver door is ajar. The key-in-ignition warning chime sounds when:

  • the driver door is ajar, the ignition lock cylinder is in the OFF/LOCK or ACC position, and the key is in the ignition lock cylinder.

The key-in-ignition warning chime stops sounding if any of the following conditions is met:

  • the driver door is closed.
  • the key is removed from the ignition lock cylinder.
  • the ignition lock cylinder is turned to the RUN position.

The key-in-ignition warning chime inputs are:

  • ignition switch position.
  • key-in-ignition warning switch (part of the ignition switch).
  • LH door ajar switch.

Headlamps On Warning Chime

The headlamps on warning chime warns that the headlamps are on when all the following conditions are met:

  • the driver door is ajar.
  • the headlamp switch is in the PARK or HEAD position.
  • the ignition lock cylinder is in the OFF/LOCK position.
  • the key is out of the ignition lock cylinder.

The headlamps on warning chime stops sounding when any one of the conditions above are removed.

The headlamps on warning chime inputs are:

  • ignition switch position.
  • headlamp switch position.
  • driver door ajar switch.
  • key-in-ignition warning switch.

Air Bag Warning Chime

The air bag warning chime warns that the air bag warning indicator is inoperative or an active fault is present within the air bag system. The audible warning format consists of 5 sets of tones in 1 second increments.

The air bag warning chime input is the RCM.

Pinpoint Tests

 

Pinpoint Test A: The Key-In-Ignition Warning Chime Does Not Operate Correctly

Normal Operation

When the key is inserted into the ignition lock cylinder, the key-in-ignition switch (part of the ignition switch) closes circuit 158 (BK/PK) to the instrument cluster. The 12 volt input signals the instrument cluster that the key is inserted. If the instrument cluster detects that the ignition lock cylinder is in the OFF position with the key inserted in the ignition cylinder and the driver door is ajar, the key-in-ignition warning chime sounds.

    Possible Causes
  • circuit 158 (BK/PK) open or short to ground
  • key-in-ignition warning switch (part of the ignition switch)
  • instrument cluster

Pinpoint Test B: The Headlamps On Warning Chime Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

The headlamps on warning chime sounds when the driver door is opened, the ignition lock cylinder is in the OFF position, the key is out, and the instrument cluster receives a headlamps/parking lamps on signal from the headlamp switch through circuit 257 (WH/RD). The headlamps on warning chime operates only when autolamp is not selected.

    Possible Causes
  • circuit 257 (WH/RD) open or short to ground
  • headlamp switch
  • instrument cluster

Pinpoint Test C: The Air Bag Warning Chime Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

The instrument cluster sounds the airbag warning chime when it receives a chime request signal from the restraints control module (RCM) through circuit 1083 (LB/PK). The RCM sends the chime request signal to the instrument cluster only if there is a fault and the air bag warning indicator is inoperative.

    Possible Causes
  • instrument cluster

Pinpoint Test D: All The Chimes Are Inoperative

Normal Operation

The warning chime is an integral part of the instrument cluster. The instrument cluster sounds the warning chimes after receiving hardwired and data signal inputs from various sources. Data input signals are transmitted to the instrument cluster over the (UART)-based protocol (UBP) communication network, circuit 1906 (PK).

    Possible Causes
  • instrument cluster

Pinpoint Test E: The Chime Sounds When The Driver Door Is Ajar (No Key In Ignition and Headlamps OFF)

Normal Operation

The instrument cluster sounds the warning chime when the driver door is open and the ignition switch is in the OFF position, or the headlamps are left on.

    Possible Causes
  • circuit 158 (BK/PK) short to voltage
  • circuit 257 (WH/RD) short to voltage
  • headlamp switch
  • key-in-ignition warning switch (part of the ignition switch)
  • instrument cluster
