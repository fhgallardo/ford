SECTION 501-20B: Supplemental Restraint System
2006 F-150/Mark LT Workshop Manual
REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION
Procedure revision date: 11/04/2005

Passenger Air Bag Module


Item Part Number Description
1 W709027-S437 Passenger air bag module bolts (3 required)
2 046B62 RH defrost grille
3 044A74 Passenger air bag module

Removal and Installation

 WARNING: Always wear safety glasses when repairing an air bag supplemental restraint system (SRS) vehicle and when handling an air bag module. This will reduce the risk of injury in the event of an accidental deployment.

 WARNING: Carry a live air bag module with the air bag and deployment door pointed away from your body. This will reduce the risk of injury in the event of an accidental deployment.

 WARNING: Do not set a live air bag module down with the deployment door face down. This will reduce the risk of injury in the event of an accidental deployment.

 WARNING: After deployment, the air bag surface can contain deposits of sodium hydroxide, a product of the gas generant combustion that is irritating to the skin. Wash your hands with soap and water afterwards.

 WARNING: Never probe the connectors on the air bag module. Doing so can result in air bag deployment, which can result in personal injury.

 WARNING: Air bag modules with discolored or damaged deployment doors must be replaced, not repainted.

 WARNING: To reduce the risk of personal injury, do not use memory saver devices.

NOTE: The air bag warning lamp illuminates when the RCM fuse is removed and the ignition switch is ON. This is normal operation and does not indicate a supplemental restraint system (SRS) fault.

NOTE: The SRS must be fully operational and free of faults before releasing the vehicle to the customer.

NOTE: Repair is made by installing a new part only. If the new part does not correct the condition, install the original part and carry out the diagnostic procedure again.

  1. Depower the system. For additional information, refer to Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) Depowering and Repowering in the General Procedures portion of this section.
  1. While pushing in on the 2 glove compartment door tabs, position the glove compartment downward.
  1. Remove the passenger air bag module bolts.
  1. Remove the RH defrost grille.
  1. Remove the 2 push pins retaining the passenger air bag module deployment door to the instrument panel (if equipped).

  1.  CAUTION: Do not handle the passenger air bag module by grabbing the edges of the deployment door.

    Reaching one hand into the glove box opening, push out on the front of the passenger air bag module, releasing the clips at the front of the bag and partially remove the passenger air bag module from the instrument panel.
  1. Disconnect the passenger air bag module electrical connectors.

Installation

  1. Connect the passenger air bag module electrical connectors.

  1. NOTE: Make sure the J-clips are in place before positioning the passenger air bag module to the instrument panel.

    Position the passenger air bag module into the instrument panel.
    1. Angle the rear of the passenger air bag module deployment door so the tabs engage to the instrument panel.
    1. Position the passenger air bag module into place and push down on the deployment door, engaging the remaining tabs.
  1. Install the RH defrost grille.
  1. Install the 2 pushpins retaining the passenger air bag module deployment door to the instrument panel (if equipped).
  1. Install the passenger air bag module bolts.
    • To install, tighten to 9 Nm (80 lb-in).
  1. Close the glove compartment.
  1. Repower the system. For additional information, refer to Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) Depowering and Repowering in the General Procedures portion of this section.
