Inspection and Verification

  1. Verify the customer concern by operating the transmission external control.
  1. Visually inspect for obvious signs of mechanical and electrical damage. Refer to the following chart:

    Visual Inspection Chart
    Mechanical Electrical
    • Damaged shift lock actuator
    • Damaged transmission control (TC) switch
    • Failed fuse(s):
      • F2.06 (15A)
      • F2.15 (5A)
      • F2.16 (10A)
    • Damaged wiring harness
    • Loose or corroded connections

  1. If the fault is not visually evident, determine the symptom.

Pinpoint Tests

 
PINPOINT TEST A: THE SHIFT CONTROL IS OUT OF CORRECT GEAR RELATIONSHIP
Test Step Result / Action to Take
  A1 CHECK SHIFT CONTROL LINKAGE  
  • Key in ON position.
  • Apply the brake pedal.
  • Gain access to the shift control linkage.
  • Actuate the transmission range selector lever in all ranges.
  • Observe all linkages during operation.
  • Is the linkage damaged?
Yes
INSTALL a new shift control linkage. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
GO to A2 .
  A2 CHECK TRANSMISSION SHIFT CABLE  
  • Check the transmission shift cable and bracket installation.
  • Is the transmission selector lever cable correctly installed and adjusted?
Yes
VERIFY the transmission selector lever cable adjustment. REFER to Selector Lever Cable Adjustment in this section. ADJUST the transmission selector lever cable if necessary. GO to A3 .

No
REPAIR as necessary. TEST the system for normal operation.
  A3 CHECK LINKAGE/CABLE FOR CORRECT GEAR RELATIONSHIP  
  • Apply the brake pedal.
  • Actuate the transmission range selector lever in all ranges.
  • Does the indicator match the gear selection?
Yes
System OK. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
REFER to Selector Lever Cable Adjustment in this section. TEST the system for normal operation.
 
PINPOINT TEST B: THE TRANSMISSION CONTROL SWITCH IS NOT OPERATING CORRECTLY
Test Step Result / Action to Take
  B1 CHECK FUSE F2.15 (5A)  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Check fuse: Central Junction Box (CJB) Fuse F2.15 (5A).
  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
REINSTALL the fuse. GO to B3 .

No
INSTALL a new fuse. TEST the system for normal operation. If the fuse fails again, GO to B2 .
  B2 CHECK TRANSMISSION CONTROL (TC) SWITCH CIRCUIT FOR SHORT TO GROUND  
  • Disconnect: Instrument Cluster Connector C220B.
  • Inspect for damaged or pushed-out pins, corrosion and loose wires. Repair as necessary.
  • Measure the resistance between TC switch connector pin 1 and ground,

  • Is the resistance greater than 10,000 ohms?
Yes
GO to B5 .

No
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.
  B3 CHECK TRANSMISSION CONTROL (TC) SWITCH CIRCUIT FOR OPEN  
  • Inspect for damaged or pushed-out pins, corrosion and loose wires. Repair as necessary.
  • Measure the resistance between the output side of F2.15 (5A) and TC switch connector pin 1.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to B4 .

No
REPAIR circuit. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.
  B4 CHECK TRANSMISSION CONTROL (TC) SWITCH FOR OPEN  
  • Measure the resistance between TC switch pins 1 and 2 while cycling the switch ON and OFF.

  • Is the resistance of the switch greater than 10,000 ohms while OFF and less than 5 ohms while ON?
Yes
GO to B6 .

No
INSTALL a new TC switch. TEST the system for normal operation.
  B5 CHECK TC SWITCH FOR SHORT TO GROUND  
  • Measure the resistance between TC switch pins 1 and 2 and ground while cycling the switch.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms at any time for either pin?
Yes
INSTALL a new transmission control switch. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
GO to B7 .
  B6 CHECK TC SWITCH CIRCUIT FOR OPEN  
  • Measure the resistance between TC switch connector pin 2 and PCM connector pin 45.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
INSTALL a new PCM. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
REPAIR circuit. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.
  B7 CHECK TC SWITCH CIRCUIT FOR SHORT TO GROUND  
  • Measure the resistance between PCM connector pin 45 and ground.

  • Is the resistance greater than 10,000 ohms?
Yes
INSTALL a new PCM. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
REPAIR circuit. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.
 
PINPOINT TEST C: THE SHIFT INTERLOCK SYSTEM DOES NOT RELEASE/LOCK CORRECTLY
Test Step Result / Action to Take
  C1 TEST FUSE F2.16 (10A)  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Check fuse: Fuse F2.16 (10A).
  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to C3 .

No
INSTALL the fuse. TEST the system for normal operation. If the fuse fails again, GO to C2 .
  C2 TEST SHIFT INTERLOCK CIRCUIT FOR SHORT TO GROUND  
  • Measure the resistance between the brake pedal position (BPP) switch connector pin 5 and ground.

  • Is the resistance greater than 10,000 ohms?
Yes
REPAIR circuit. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
GO to C3 .
  C3 TEST SHIFT INTERLOCK CIRCUIT FOR OPEN  
  • Measure the resistance between the output side of fuse F2.16 (10A) and brake pedal position (BPP) switch connector pin 5.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to C4 .

No
REPAIR circuit. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.
  C4 TEST BRAKE PEDAL POSITION (BPP) SWITCH  
  • Measure the resistance between the BPP switch, pin 5 and pin 6 while open (OFF) and closed (ON).

  • Is the resistance of the switch greater than 10,000 ohms while OFF and less than 5 ohms while ON?
Yes
GO to C5 .

No
INSTALL a new BPP switch. TEST the system for normal operation.
  C5 TEST SHIFT INTERLOCK SOLENOID CIRCUIT FOR SHORT TO GROUND  
  • Disconnect: Brake Pedal Position (BPP) Switch .
  • For vehicles equipped with a column shifter, measure the resistance between shift interlock solenoid connector pin 2 and ground.

  • For vehicles equipped with a floor shifter, measure the resistance between the shift control electrical connector pin 1 and ground.

  • Is the resistance greater than 10,000 ohms?
Yes
REPAIR circuit. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
GO to C6 .
  C6 TEST SHIFT INTERLOCK ACTUATOR CIRCUIT FOR OPEN  
  • For vehicles equipped with a column shifter, measure the resistance between the shift interlock actuator connector pin 2 and the BPP switch connector pin 6.

  • For vehicles equipped with a floor shifter, measure the resistance between the shift control electrical connector pin 1 and the BPP switch connector pin 6.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
If equipped with a column shifter, GO to C10 . If equipped with a floor shifter, GO to C7 .

No
REPAIR circuit. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.
  C7 TEST FUSE F2.15 (5A)  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Check fuse: F2.15 (5A).
  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to C8 .

No
INSTALL the fuse and TEST the system for normal operations. If the fuse fails again, GO to C8 .
  C8 TEST FLOOR SHIFTER CIRCUIT FOR SHORT TO GROUND  
  • Measure the resistance between the shift control electrical connector pin 2 and ground.

  • Is the resistance greater than 10,000 ohms?
Yes
REPAIR circuit. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
GO to C9 .
  C9 TEST FLOOR SHIFTER CIRCUIT FOR OPEN  
  • Measure the resistance between the output side of fuse F2.15 (5A) and shift control electrical connector pin 2.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to C10 .

No
REPAIR circuit. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.
  C10 TEST SHIFT INTERLOCK ACTUATOR GROUND CIRCUIT FOR OPEN  
  • If equipped with a column shifter, measure the resistance between shift interlock actuator connector pin 4 and ground.

  • If equipped with a floor shifter, measure the resistance between shift control electrical connector pin 6 and ground.

  • Is the resistance 5 ohms or less?
Yes
INSTALL a new shift lock actuator. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
REPAIR circuit. RECONNECT all components. TEST the system for normal operation.
