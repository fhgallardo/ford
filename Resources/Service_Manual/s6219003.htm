Principles of Operation

There are 2 exterior rear view mirror options, a manually adjustable paddle-style mirror or a power adjustable aero-style mirror. Power mirrors allow the driver or passenger mirror glass to be positioned automatically. The position of the power mirror glass is controlled by the exterior mirror control switch, which is located on the LH front door trim panel. Adjusting the exterior mirror control switch to the LH or RH position determines which power mirror glass will be controlled. If equipped with power folding mirrors, the center position of the exterior mirror control switch allows the power folding mirrors to be folded or unfolded.

The power exterior rear view mirrors are available with a heated glass option, which heats the mirror glass to remove frost, snow, ice and condensation. On vehicles with optional rear window defrost, the heated exterior rear view mirrors are heated for a period of 10 minutes when the rear window defrost switch is pressed.

The power exterior rear view mirrors are available with a power folding option, which allows both exterior mirrors to be extended or retracted from the exterior mirror control switch.

Heated Exterior Rear View Mirror

On vehicles with optional rear window defrost, the heated exterior mirror feature will only function when the rear window defrost is operating. There is no separate control for heated exterior mirrors; they function anytime the rear window defrost is ON. The heated mirror power is supplied by the rear window defrost relay through a separate central junction box (CJB) fuse that isolates the heated mirrors from the rear window defrost in the event of a concern.

Auto-Dimming Mirror

The interior rear view mirror may be equipped with an auto-dimming option. This feature automatically reduces the glare caused by headlamps reflecting in the mirror. The auto-dimming feature is disabled when the vehicle is in REVERSE. Power is supplied to the auto-dimming interior mirror when the ignition is in the RUN or ACC position.

The automatic dimming feature has 2 photoelectric sensors that detect forward and rear light conditions. Based on these inputs, the automatic dimming feature adjusts the reflectance level of the mirror glass to eliminate unwanted glare. The reflectance level of the mirror glass is variable and depends on the amount of rear glare in relation to ambient light conditions in front of the interior mirror.

When the forward sensor detects daytime conditions, the rear sensor is inactive and the mirror stays in a high reflectance mode. When the forward sensor detects nighttime conditions, the rear sensor is active and detects glare from the headlights of vehicles approaching from the rear or other glare-producing light sources. To provide increased visibility when backing up, the mirror will automatically return to a high reflectance mode whenever the shifter is placed in REVERSE.

If the forward or rear sensors are blocked, the automatic dimming interior mirror might not work correctly.

Power Folding Mirrors

NOTE: The power folding mirrors must be synchronized any time the mirrors are folded or unfolded without using the mirror control switch, or if a new power folding mirror has been installed. Refer to Exterior Mirror Motor Synchronization — Power Folding in this section.

The power folding mirror option allows both power mirrors to be folded or unfolded from the exterior mirror control switch. When the exterior mirror switch is set to the power folding mirror (center) position and pressed rearward and released, the mirrors will fold in if they are folded out, or unfold if they are folded in. The power folding mirrors contain a drive motor which is not replaceable. If a power fold motor is inoperative, a new power folding mirror assembly must be installed. The mirror glass motor, mirror glass, turn signal indicator and puddle lamp are the only replaceable components in a power folding mirror. If the power folding mirrors are folded or unfolded continuously (6 to 10 cycles), the power lockout feature will disable the system for 2 minutes to prevent damage to the power fold motors. After 2 minutes have elapsed, normal operation will resume.

Pinpoint Tests

Pinpoint Test A: The Mirrors Are Inoperative

Normal Operation

Under normal operation, the exterior mirror control switch receives power through circuit 956 (OG/LG) and ground through circuit 57 (BK). The exterior mirror control switch uses circuit 542 (YE) as the common circuit for both exterior mirror UP/DOWN and RIGHT/LEFT movement.

    Possible Causes
  • Open in circuits 956 (OG/LG) or 57 (BK)
  • Open, short to ground or short to power in circuit 542 (YE)
  • Inoperative exterior mirror control switch
  • Fuse

Pinpoint Test C: A Single Mirror Does Not Function With Switch Logic

Normal Operation

Under normal operation, the exterior mirror control switch uses circuits 540 (RD), 541 (DB), 542 (YE), 543 (DG) or 544 (VT) to control the exterior mirror movement.

    Normal Operation
  • When power is received through circuit 540 (RD)/543 (DG) and ground through circuit 542 (YE), the LH/RH exterior mirror will operate LEFT.
  • When ground is received through circuit 540 (RD)/543 (DG) and power through circuit 542 (YE), the LH/RH exterior mirror will operate RIGHT.
  • When power is received through circuit 541 (DB)/544 (VT) and ground through circuit 542 (YE), the LH/RH exterior mirror will operate DOWN.
  • When ground is received through circuit 541 (DB)/544 (VT) and power through circuit 542 (YE), the LH/RH exterior mirror will operate UP.
    Possible Causes
  • Open in circuits 540 (RD), 541 (DB), 543 (DG) or 544 (VT)
  • Inoperative exterior mirror control switch
  • Inoperative exterior mirror motor

Pinpoint Test D: The Auto-Dimming Mirror Does Not Operate Correctly

Normal Operation

Under normal operation, the auto-dimming mirror receives power from circuit 294 (WH/LB) and ground from circuit 57 (BK). If the vehicle is placed in REVERSE, voltage is sent to circuit 140 (BK/PK) and the auto-dimming mirror will turn the dimming feature off. There are 2 photoelectric sensors: one in the front of the interior rear view mirror and one mounted on the glass side of the mirror. If the sensors are blocked, the auto-dimming feature might not work correctly. Always verify both sensors are not physically blocked before attempting to diagnose auto-dimming mirror concerns.

    Possible Causes
  • Open in circuit 294 (WH/LB), 57 (BK) or 140 (BK/PK)
  • Inoperative auto-dimming mirror

Pinpoint Test H: The Power Folding Mirrors Do Not Operate/Do Not Operate Correctly

NOTE: The power folding mirrors must be synchronized anytime the RH, LH or both power folding mirrors are folded or unfolded without using the power folding mirror control switch, or if a new power folding mirror has been installed. Refer to Exterior Mirror Motor Synchronization — Power Folding in this section.

NOTE: If the power folding mirrors are folded or unfolded continuously (6-10 cycles), the power folding mirror module will disable the system for 2 minutes to prevent damage to the power folding mirror motors. After 2 minutes have elapsed, normal operation will resume.

Refer to Wiring Diagrams Cell 124 , Power Mirrors for schematic and connector information.

Normal Operation

Under normal operation, the power folding mirror module receives power from circuit 720 (RD/YE) and ground from circuit 57 (BK). The power folding mirrors use circuits 1037 (VT/LG) and 1038 (VT/YE) as common circuits. When the exterior mirror select switch is set to the center position and then pressed rearward, ground is supplied to circuit 2281 (VT). When the power folding mirror module receives a ground signal on circuit 2281 (VT), the power folding mirror module will activate the power folding mirrors in the opposite direction of the last activation for approximately 20 seconds. When the power folding mirrors are positioned outwards and ground is supplied to circuit 2281 (VT), the power folding mirror module will ground circuit 1038 (VT/YE) and supply voltage to circuit 1037 (VT/LG) and the power folding mirror motors will move the LH and RH power folding mirrors inwards. When the power folding mirrors are positioned inwards and ground is supplied to circuit 2281 (VT), the power folding mirror module will ground circuit 1037 (VT/LG) and supply voltage to circuit 1038 (VT/YE) and the power folding mirror motors will move the LH and RH mirrors outwards.

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse(s)
  • Wiring, terminals or connectors
  • Exterior mirror control switch
  • Power folding mirror module
  • Power folding mirror
 
PINPOINT TEST H: THE POWER FOLDING MIRRORS DO NOT OPERATE/DO NOT OPERATE CORRECTLY
Test Step Result / Action to Take
  H1 CHECK POWER FOLDING MIRROR OPERATION  
  • NOTE: Set the exterior mirror control switch to the center position.
  • Press the exterior mirror control switch rearward to activate the power folding mirrors.
  • Are both power folding mirrors inoperative?
Yes
GO to H2 .

No
GO to H8 .
  H2 CHECK THE EXTERIOR MIRROR CONTROL SWITCH  
  • Disconnect: Exterior Mirror Control Switch C527.
  • Carry out the exterior mirror switch component test.

    Refer to Wiring Diagrams Cell 149 for schematic and connector information.

  • Is the exterior mirror control switch OK?
Yes
GO to H3 .

No
INSTALL a new exterior mirror control switch. REFER to Exterior Mirror Control Switch in this section.
  H3 CHECK CIRCUIT 2281 (VT) FOR A SHORT TO GROUND  
  • Disconnect: Power Folding Mirror Module C3276.
  • Measure the resistance between exterior mirror control switch C527-4, circuit 2281 (VT) harness side and ground.

  • Is the resistance greater than 10,000 ohms?
Yes
GO to H4 .

No
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.
  H4 CHECK CIRCUIT 2281 (VT) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Measure the resistance between exterior mirror switch C527-4, circuit 2281 (VT) harness side and power folding mirror module C3276-4, circuit 2281 (VT) harness side.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to H5 .

No
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.
  H5 CHECK FOR POWER TO THE POWER FOLDING MIRROR MODULE  
  • Measure the voltage between power folding mirror module C3276-3, circuit 720 (RD/YE) harness side and ground.

  • Is the voltage greater than 10 volts?
Yes
GO to H6 .

No
VERIFY that central junction box (CJB) fuse F4 (10A) is OK. If OK, REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.
  H6 CHECK CIRCUIT 57 (BK) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Measure the resistance between power folding mirror module C3276-1, circuit 57 (BK) harness side and ground.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to H7 .

No
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.
  H7 CHECK CIRCUIT 1037 (VT/LG) AND CIRCUIT 1038 (VT/YE) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Disconnect: LH Exterior Mirror C521.
  • Measure the resistance between LH exterior mirror:
    • C521-7, circuit 1037 (VT/LG) harness side and power folding mirror module C3276-2, circuit 1037 (VT/LG) harness side.
    • C521-16, circuit 1038 (VT/YE) harness side and power folding mirror module C3276-5, circuit 1038 (VT/YE) harness side.

  • Are the resistances less than 5 ohms?
Yes
INSTALL a new power folding mirror module. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
REPAIR the circuit(s). TEST the system for normal operation.
  H8 CHECK THE POWER FOLDING MIRROR MODULE  
  • Disconnect: Inoperative Exterior Mirror C521 or C615.
  • NOTE: LH mirror only.
  • Measure the resistance between ground and LH exterior mirror:
    • C521-7, circuit 1037 (VT/LG) harness side.
    • C521-16, circuit 1038 (VT/YE) harness side.
  • NOTE: RH mirror only.
  • Measure the resistance between ground and RH exterior mirror:
    • C615-7, circuit 1037 (VT/LG) harness side.
    • C615-16, circuit 1038 (VT/YE) harness side.

  • Are the resistances less than 5 ohms?
Yes
INSTALL a new LH or RH exterior mirror. REFER to Exterior Mirror in this section. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
GO to H9 .
  H9 CHECK CIRCUIT 1037 (VT/LG) AND CIRCUIT 1038 (VT/YE) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Disconnect: Power Folding Mirror Module C3276.
  • NOTE: LH mirror only.
  • Measure the resistance between LH exterior mirror:
    • C521-7, circuit 1037 (VT/LG) harness side and power folding module C3276-2, circuit 1037 (VT/LG) harness side.
    • C521-16, circuit 1038 (VT/YE) harness side and power folding module C3276-5, circuit 1038 (VT/YE) harness side.
  • NOTE: RH mirror only.
  • Measure the resistance between RH exterior mirror:
    • C615-7, circuit 1037 (VT/LG) harness side and power folding module C3276-2, circuit 1037 (VT/LG) harness side.
    • C615-16, circuit 1038 (VT/YE) harness side and power folding module C3276-5, circuit 1038 (VT/YE) harness side.

  • Are the resistances less than 5 ohms?
Yes
INSTALL a new power folding mirror module. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
REPAIR the circuit(s). TEST the system for normal operation.
