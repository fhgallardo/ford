Principles of Operation

Dimmable Illumination

With the parking lamps ON, voltage is supplied to the instrument panel dimmer switch (part of the headlamp switch). The instrument panel dimmer switch then sends a variable pulse width modulated (PWM) voltage to the instrument cluster and other dimmable switches and components.

Fixed Illumination

With the ignition switch in the START or RUN position, the delay accessory relay provides voltage to the non-dimmable switches. The delay accessory relay is controlled through the instrument cluster.

Pinpoint Tests

 

Pinpoint Test A: The Control Illumination Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

With the parking lamps ON, voltage is supplied to the dimmer switch (part of the headlamp switch) through circuit 14 (BN). The dimmer switch (part of the headlamp switch) then sends a variable pulse width modulated (PWM) voltage to the dimmable backlighting components through circuit 19 (LB/RD). Ground is supplied to the dimmer switch (part of the headlamp switch) through circuit 57 (BK).

    Possible Causes
  • fuse
  • circuit 14 (BN) open
  • circuit 19 (LB/RD) open or short to ground
  • circuit 57 (BK) open
  • headlamp switch
 
PINPOINT TEST A: THE CONTROL ILLUMINATION IS INOPERATIVE
Test Step Result / Action to Take
  A1 CHECK THE PARKING LAMPS OPERATION  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Turn the parking lamps ON.
  • Do the parking lamps illuminate?
Yes
GO to A2 .

No
VERIFY the central junction (CJB) box fuse 6 (15A) is OK. If OK, REFER to Section 417-01 to continue diagnosis of the parking lamps.
  A2 CHECK CIRCUIT 14 (BN) FOR VOLTAGE  
  • Turn the parking lamps OFF.
  • Disconnect: Headlamp Switch C2150b.
  • Turn the parking lamps ON.
  • Measure the voltage between the headlamp switch C2150b-6, circuit 14 (BN), harness side and ground.

  • Is the voltage greater than 10 volts?
Yes
GO to A3 .

No
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.
  A3 CHECK CIRCUIT 57 (BK) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Turn the parking lamps OFF.
  • Measure the resistance between the headlamp switch C2150b-4, circuit 57 (BK), harness side and ground.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to A4 .

No
REPAIR circuit 57 (BK). TEST the system for normal operation.
  A4 CHECK CIRCUIT 19 (LB/RD) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Disconnect: Headlamp Switch C2150a.
  • Measure the resistance between the headlamp switch C2150b-1, circuit 19 (LB/RD), harness side and the headlamp switch C2150a-8, circuit 19 (LB/RD), harness side.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
INSTALL a new headlamp switch. REFER to Section 417-01 .

No
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.
 

Pinpoint Test B: The Instrument Cluster Illumination Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

The dimmer switch (part of the headlamp switch) sends a variable pulse width modulated (PWM) voltage to the instrument cluster through circuit 19 (LB/RD). The instrument cluster is grounded through circuit 57 (BK).

    Possible Causes
  • circuit 19 (LB/RD) open
  • circuit 57 (BK) open
  • instrument cluster

  • Turn the parking lamps ON, and rotate the dimmer switch (part of the headlamp switch) to the full illumination position.
  • Measure the voltage between the instrument cluster C220a-1, circuit 19 (LB/RD), harness side and ground.

  • Is the voltage greater than 10 volts?
Yes
GO to B2 .

No
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.
  B2 CHECK CIRCUIT 57 (BK) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Turn the parking lamps OFF.
  • Measure the resistance between the instrument cluster C220a-17, circuit 57 (BK), harness side and ground.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to B3 .

No
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.
  B3 CHECK FOR CORRECT INSTRUMENT CLUSTER OPERATION  
  • Disconnect all the instrument cluster connectors.
  • Check for:
    • corrosion
    • pushed-out pins
  • Connect all the instrument cluster connectors and make sure they seat correctly.
  • Operate the system and verify the concern is still present.

Yes

Pinpoint Test C: The Climate Control Panel Illumination Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

A variable pulse width modulated (PWM) voltage is supplied to the climate control assembly through circuit 19 (LB/RD). Ground is supplied to the climate control assembly through circuit 875 (BK/LB).

    Possible Causes
  • circuit 19 (LB/RD) open
  • circuit 875 (BK/LB) open
  • climate control assembly

Pinpoint Test D: A Single Illumination Source Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

For dimmable backlighting, a variable pulse width modulated (PWM) voltage is supplied to the illumination source(s) through circuit 19 (LB/RD).

For non-dimmable backlighting, with the ignition switch in the START or RUN position, voltage is supplied to the illumination sources from the accessory delay relay through circuit 54 (LG/YE).

Ground is supplied to the illumination source(s) through circuit 57 (BK).

    Possible Causes
  • circuit 19 (LB/RD) open
  • circuit 54 (LG/YE) open
  • circuit 57 (BK) open
  • message center switch
  • parking aid switch
  • adjustable pedal switch
  • audio unit
  • four-wheel drive switch
  • floor shifter
  • digital versatile disc (DVD) player
  • roof opening panel control switch
  • door lock control switch(es)

Pinpoint Test E: The Steering Wheel Control Switch Illumination Source Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

Variable pulse width modulated (PWM) voltage is supplied to the steering wheel audio and climate control switches and the speed control switches through circuit 1011 (TN/OG). Ground is supplied to the steering wheel control switch from the powertrain control module (PCM) through circuit 848 (DG/OG).

    Possible Causes
  • fuse
  • circuit 848 (DG/OG) open
  • circuit 1011 (TN/OG) open
  • clockspring
  • steering wheel audio and climate control switches
  • speed control switches
  • steering wheel control switches harness
 
PINPOINT TEST E: THE STEERING WHEEL CONTROL SWITCH ILLUMINATION SOURCE IS INOPERATIVE

  • Turn the parking lamps ON, and rotate the dimmer switch (part of the headlamp switch) to the full illumination position.
  • Measure the voltage between the clockspring C218b-1, circuit 1011 (TN/OG), harness side and ground.

  • Is the voltage greater than 10 volts?
Yes
GO to E3 .

No

Pinpoint Test F: The Instrument Panel Illumination Does Not Dim

Normal Operation

Variable pulse width modulated (PWM) voltage is supplied to the instrument cluster and panel illumination source(s) through circuit 19 (LB/RD).

    Possible Causes
  • circuit 19 (LB/RD) short to voltage
  • instrument panel dimmer switch (part of the headlamp switch)
 
PINPOINT TEST F: THE INSTRUMENT PANEL ILLUMINATION DOES NOT DIM
Test Step Result / Action to Take
  F1 CHECK CIRCUIT 19 (LB/RD) FOR A SHORT TO VOLTAGE  
  • Turn the parking lamps OFF.
  • Disconnect: Headlamp Switch C2150b.
  • Monitor the instrument cluster illumination.
  • Does the instrument panel remain illuminated?
Yes
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
INSTALL a new headlamp switch. REFER to Section 417-01 . TEST the system for normal operation.
 

Pinpoint Test G: The Non-Dimmable Illumination Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

When the instrument cluster senses that the ignition switch is in the START or RUN position, it provides a ground for the delay accessory relay coil through circuit 1332 (LB). The delay accessory relay provides switched voltage to the non-dimmable components through circuits 54 (LG/YE) and 400 (LB/BK).

    Possible Causes
  • circuit 1332 (LB) open
  • delay accessory relay
  • central junction box (CJB)
  • instrument cluster
 
PINPOINT TEST G: THE NON-DIMMABLE ILLUMINATION IS INOPERATIVE

 CAUTION: Use the correct probe adaptor(s) when making measurements. Failure to use the correct probe adaptor(s) may damage the connector.

Test Step Result / Action to Take
  G1 CHECK DELAY ACCESSORY RELAY COIL GROUND CIRCUIT  
  • Disconnect: CJB C270e.
  • Key in ON position.
  • Measure the resistance between the CJB C270e-21, circuit 1332 (LB), harness side and ground.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to G2 .

No
GO to G3 .
  G2 CHECK THE DELAY ACCESSORY RELAY  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Carry out the Mini - ISO Relay Test for the delay accessory relay.

    Refer to Wiring Diagrams Cell 149 for schematic and connector information.

  • Is the delay accessory relay OK?
Yes
REPAIR or INSTALL a new CJB. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
INSTALL a new delay accessory relay. TEST the system for normal operation.
  G3 CHECK CIRCUIT 1332 (LB) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Disconnect: Instrument Cluster C220b.
  • Measure the resistance between the CJB C270e-21, circuit 1332 (LB), harness side and the instrument cluster C220b-5, circuit 1332 (LB) harness side.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
REFER to Section 413-01 for continued diagnosis of the instrument cluster.

No
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.
