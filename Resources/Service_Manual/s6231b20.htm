Removal

All vehicles

  1. With the vehicle in NEUTRAL, position it on a hoist. For additional information, refer to Section 100-02 .
  1. Drain the engine oil.
  1. If equipped, remove the 4 skid plate bolts and the skid plate.
  1. Remove the bolts and the crossmember.

4WD vehicles

  1. Position a suitable hydraulic jack under the front axle. Securely strap the jack to the axle.

  1. NOTE: Rotate the steering column so the pinch bolt for the steering column coupling allows clearance for the isolator bolt.

    Remove the upper front axle carrier mounting bushing bolt.
  1. Remove the axle shaft housing carrier bushing bolt.
  1. Remove the lower front axle carrier mounting bushing bolt.
  1. Partially lower the front axle assembly.

All vehicles

  1.  CAUTION: Do not use metal scrapers, wire brushes, power abrasive discs or other abrasive means to clean the sealing surfaces. These may cause scratches and gouges resulting in leak paths. Use a plastic scraper to clean the sealing surfaces.

    Remove the 16 oil pan bolts and the oil pan, discard the oil pan gasket. Clean the sealing surfaces with silicone gasket remover and metal surface prep. Follow the directions on the packaging. Inspect the mating surfaces.

Installation

All vehicles

  1. NOTE: If the oil pan is not secured within 4 minutes, the sealant must be removed and the sealing area cleaned with silicone gasket remover and metal surface prep. Follow the directions on the packaging. Allow to dry until there is no sign of wetness, or 4 minutes, whichever is longer. Failure to follow this procedure can cause future oil leakage.

    Apply the silicone gasket and sealant at the engine front cover-to-cylinder block mating surface.

  1. NOTE: If the oil pan is not secured within 4 minutes, the sealant must be removed and the sealing area cleaned with silicone gasket remover and metal surface prep. Follow the directions on the packaging. Allow to dry until there is no sign of wetness, or 4 minutes, whichever is longer. Failure to follow this procedure can cause future oil leakage.

    Apply silicone gasket and sealant in the locations shown.
  1. Position the new oil pan gasket and the oil pan and loosely install the 16 bolts.
  1. Tighten the bolts in 3 stages in the sequence shown.
    • Stage 1: Tighten to 2 Nm (18 lb-in).
    • Stage 2: Tighten to 20 Nm (15 lb-ft).
    • Stage 3: Tighten an additional 60 degrees.

4WD vehicles

  1. Position the front axle.

  1. NOTE: Rotate the steering column so the pinch bolt for the steering column coupling allows clearance for the isolator bolt.

    Install the upper front axle carrier mounting bushing bolt.
    • Tighten to 90 Nm (66 lb-ft).
  1. Install the axle shaft housing carrier bushing nut and bolt.
    • Tighten to 90 Nm (66 lb-ft).
  1. Install the lower front axle carrier mounting bushing bolt.
    • Tighten to 90 Nm (66 lb-ft).

All vehicles

  1. Install the crossmember and the 4 bolts.
    • Tighten to 90 Nm (66 lb-ft).
  1. If equipped, install the skid plate and the 4 bolts.
    • Tighten to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).
  1. Install the engine oil pan drain plug and fill the engine with clean engine oil.
    • Tighten to 14 Nm (10 lb-ft).
