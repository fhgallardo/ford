Trailer Lamps

Refer to Wiring Diagrams Cell 95 for schematic and connector information.

Principles of Operation

The trailer tow stop/turn lamps receive voltage from the rear stop/turn lamp circuits. When a rear stop/turn lamp is illuminated, voltage is also sent to the trailer tow connector. The trailer tow parking lamps are supplied voltage by the trailer tow parking lamp relay. When the parking lamps are illuminated, voltage is also sent to the trailer tow parking lamp relay coil. When the trailer tow parking lamp relay is energized, voltage is routed to the trailer tow connector. The trailer tow reversing lamps are supplied voltage from the reversing lamp circuit. When the reversing lamps are illuminated, voltage is supplied to the trailer tow connector.

 

Inspection and Verification

  1. Verify the customer concern.
  1. Verify the exterior lighting system of the vehicle is operating correctly. If not, refer to the appropriate pinpoint test in this section.
  1. Visually inspect the following for obvious signs of electrical damage.

    Visual Inspection Chart
    Electrical
    • Central junction box (CJB) fuse(s):
      • 10 (20A) (trailer tow parking and reversing lamps)
      • 36 (10A) (trailer tow RH stop/turn)
      • 42 (10A) (trailer tow LH stop/turn)
      • 105 (30A) (trailer tow electric brakes)
      • 106 (30A) (trailer tow battery charging)
    • Trailer brake control module
    • Circuity
    • Relay(s)
    • Bulb(s)

NOTE: When diagnosing the trailer tow lamps, begin by verifying that the vehicle lamp systems are all functioning correctly with the trailer disconnected.

  1. If an obvious cause for an observed or reported concern is found, correct the cause (if possible) before proceeding to the next step.
  1. If the concern is not visually evident, verify the symptom. GO to Symptom Chart .

Pinpoint Tests

 

Pinpoint Test V: The Trailer Lamps Are Inoperative

Normal Operation

Circuit 14 (BN) supplies voltage to the exterior lamps and the coil side of the trailer tow parking lamp relay. Ground is supplied to the trailer tow parking lamp relay through the central junction box (CJB). When the trailer tow parking lamp relay is energized, voltage is routed from the CJB to the trailer tow connector through circuit 962 (BN/WH). Circuit 140 (BK/PK) supplies voltage to the trailer tow connector for the trailer to reversing lamps. Circuits 52 (YE) (LH stop/turn) and 64 (DG) (RH stop/turn) are spliced to circuits 9 (LG/OG) and 5 (OG/LB), respectively within the CJB. Ground is supplied to the trailer through circuit 206 (WH).

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Circuit 14 (BN) open
  • Circuit 52 (YE) open
  • Circuit 64 (DG) open
  • Circuit 140 (BK/PK) open
  • Circuit 206 (WH) open
  • Circuit 962 (BN/WH) open
  • Trailer tow parking lamp relay
  • CJB
  • Trailer

Pinpoint Test W: The Trailer Lamps Are On Continuously

Normal Operation

The parking lamps are controlled by the trailer tow parking lamp relay. When the trailer tow parking lamp relay is energized, voltage is routed from the central junction box (CJB) to the trailer tow connector through circuit 962 (BN/WH). The reversing lamps are controlled by the trailer tow reversing lamp relay. When the trailer tow reversing lamp relay is energized, voltage is routed from the central junction box (CJB) to the trailer tow connector through circuit 140 (BK/PK).

    Possible Causes
  • Circuit 140 (BK/PK) short to voltage
  • Circuit 962 (BN/WH) short to voltage
  • Trailer tow parking lamp relay
  • Trailer tow reversing lamp relay
  • CJB
  • Trailer

Pinpoint Test X: The Trailer Battery Charging Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

The trailer battery charging is accomplished through the use of the trailer tow battery charge relay. The trailer tow battery charge relay is supplied voltage and ground through the central junction box (CJB). When energized, the trailer tow battery charge relay routes voltage from the CJB to the trailer tow connector through circuit 49 (OG).

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Circuit 49 (OG) open
  • Trailer tow battery charge relay
  • CJB
  • Trailer

Pinpoint Test Y: The Trailer Electric Brakes Are Inoperative/Do Not Operate Correctly

Normal Operation

The trailer brake control module receives battery voltage through circuit 50 (RD) and provides output voltage to the trailer tow connector through circuit 43 (DB). The trailer brake control module is grounded through circuit 57 (BK). The trailer brake control module receives voltage input when the parking lamps are illuminated through circuit 14 (BN). When the brake pedal is applied, the trailer brake control module receives voltage input through circuit 511 (LG).

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Circuit 14 (BN) open
  • Circuit 43 (DB) open, short to voltage or ground
  • Circuit 50 (RD) open
  • Circuit 57 (BK) open
  • Circuit 511 (LG) open, short to voltage or ground
  • Trailer brake control module
  • Trailer
