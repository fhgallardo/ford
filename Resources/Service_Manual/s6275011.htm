SECTION 307-05: Automatic Transaxle/Transmission External Controls
2006 F-150/Mark LT Workshop Manual
REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION
Procedure revision date: 01/05/2006

Selector Lever Cable — Column Shift

Removal

  1. With the vehicle in NEUTRAL, position it on a hoist. For additional information, refer to Section 100-02 .

  1. NOTE: 4.6/5.4L shown, 4.2L similar.

    Disconnect the shift cable from the transmission.
    1. Disconnect the cable from the manual lever.
    1. Disconnect the cable from the cable bracket.
  1. Remove the selector lever cable from the routing clip.
  1. Remove the 2 screws and the lower dash panel.
  1. Disconnect the shift cable from the steering column.
    1. Disconnect the shift cable from the transmission selector lever arm and support.
    1. Disconnect the shift cable from the steering column bracket.
    1. Remove and discard the shift cable retainer.
  1. Use a suitable tool to push the rubber grommet through the bulkhead.
  1. From the passenger compartment, remove the shift cable from the vehicle.

Installation

  1. From inside the vehicle, feed the shift cable through the opening in the bulkhead and downward toward the transmission.
  1. Seat the shift cable rubber grommet in the bulkhead opening.
  1. Connect the shift cable to the steering column.
    1. Install the new shift cable retainer.
    1. Connect the shift cable to the steering column bracket.
    1. Connect the shift cable to the transmission selector lever arm and support.
  1. Place the gearshift lever in the (D) position.
  1. Hang a 1.4 kg (3 lb) weight on the gearshift lever.
  1. Rotate the manual lever forward until the travel stops. Then rotate the manual lever back 2 detents to the (D) position.

  1. NOTE: 4.6/5.4L shown, 4.2L similar.

    Connect the shift cable to the shift cable bracket. Do not connect the shift cable to the manual control lever at this time.
  1. Connect the shift cable to the manual control lever and lock the adjuster.

  1.  CAUTION: Failure to route the selector lever cable in the routing clip can allow the cable to rest on exhaust components causing damage to the cable.

    Route the selector lever cable in the routing clip.
  1. Verify the vehicle will start in PARK and NEUTRAL. Verify the reverse lamps illuminate in the REVERSE position. If not, check the adjustment of the transmission range (TR) sensor. For additional information, refer to Section 307-01 .
  1. Install the 2 screws and the lower dash panel.
