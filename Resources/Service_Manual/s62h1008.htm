Fog Lamps

Refer to Wiring Diagrams Cell 86 for schematic and connector information.


Special Tool(s)
73III Automotive Meter
105-R0057 or equivalent
 

Principles of Operation

The fog lamp switch is integral to the headlamp switch. When the fog lamp switch is engaged, voltage is routed to the fog lamp relay coil, which then energizes and routes voltage to the fog lamps. The fog lamp relay coil uses the high beam circuit for ground. When the high beams are turned on, the fog lamp relay will not energize.

  1. Verify the customer concern.
  1. Visually inspect the following for obvious signs of mechanical or electrical damage.

Pinpoint Tests

 

Pinpoint Test R: The Fog Lamps Are Inoperative

Normal Operation

The fog lamps receive voltage from the fog lamp relay (when energized) through circuit 478 (TN/OG). The fog lamp switch (integral to the headlamp switch) is supplied voltage through circuit 14 (BN) when the parking lamps are turned on. When the fog lamp switch is engaged, voltage is routed to circuit 188 (WH/BK) which flows to the fog lamp relay coil. The fog lamp relay coil is grounded through the high beam voltage supply circuit (circuit 12 [LG/BK]). When the fog lamp relay is energized, the relay routes voltage from the central junction box (CJB) to circuit 478 (TN/OG) which supplies voltage to the fog lamps. When the high beam relay is energized, voltage on circuit 12 (LG/BK) interrupts the fog lamp relay so that the fog lamps will not illuminate.

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Open high beam circuit
  • Circuit 14 (BN) open
  • Circuit 188 (WH/BK) open
  • Circuit 478 (TN/OG) open
  • Fog lamp relay
  • Headlamp switch
  • CJB

Test Step Result / Action to Take

No

  R2 CHECK THE FOG LAMP RELAY  
  • Disconnect: Fog Lamp Relay C2241.
  • Carry out the fog lamp relay component test.

    Refer to Wiring Diagrams Cell 149 for schematic and connector information.

  • Is the relay OK?
Yes
GO to R3 .

No
INSTALL a new fog lamp relay. TEST the system for normal operation.
  R3 CHECK FOR VOLTAGE TO THE FOG LAMP RELAY  
  • Place the headlamp switch in the PARKING LAMPS ON position and engage the fog lamp switch.
  • Measure the voltage between the fog lamp relay C2241-2, circuit 188 (WH/BK), harness side and ground.

  • Is the voltage greater than 10 volts?
Yes
TURN the headlamp switch off. GO to R4 .

No
TURN the headlamp switch off. GO to R6 .
  R4 CHECK THE CJB  
  • Connect a fused (15A) jumper wire between the fog lamp relay C2241-3, harness side and the fog lamp relay C2241-5, circuit 478 (TN/OG), harness side.

  • Do the fog lamps illuminate?
Yes
REPAIR or INSTALL a new CJB. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
LEAVE the jumper wire connected. GO to R5 .
  R5 CHECK CIRCUIT 478 (TN/OG) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Disconnect: CJB C270a.
  • Measure the voltage between the CJB C270a pin 12, circuit 478 (TN/OG), component side and ground.

  • Is the voltage greater than 10 volts?
Yes
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
VERIFY the CJB fuse 17 (15A) is OK. If OK, REPAIR or INSTALL a new CJB. TEST the system for normal operation.
  R6 CHECK THE HEADLAMP SWITCH OUTPUT  
  • Disconnect: CJB C270h.
  • Place the headlamp switch in the PARKING LAMPS ON position and engage the fog lamp switch.
  • Measure the voltage between the CJB C270h-5, circuit 188 (WH/BK), harness side and ground.

  • Is the voltage greater than 10 volts?
Yes
REPAIR or INSTALL a new CJB. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
TURN the headlamp switch off. GO to R7 .
  R7 CHECK THE HEADLAMP SWITCH  
  • Carry out the headlamp switch component test.

    Refer to Wiring Diagrams Cell 149 for schematic and connector information.

  • Is the headlamp switch OK?
Yes
REPAIR circuit 188 (WH/BK). TEST the system for normal operation.

No
INSTALL a new headlamp switch. REFER to Headlamp Switch in this section. TEST the system for normal operation.
