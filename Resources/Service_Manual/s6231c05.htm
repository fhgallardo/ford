Removal

  1. Recover the refrigerant. For additional information, refer to Section 412-00 .
  1. Remove the powertrain control module (PCM) and bracket. For additional information, refer to Section 303-14 .
  1. Remove the RH ignition coils. For additional information, refer to Section 303-07C .
  1. Remove the nut and disconnect the A/C compressor manifold tube.
    • Discard the O-ring seals.
  1. Remove the suction accumulator-to-A/C compressor manifold tube stud.
  1. Disconnect the evaporator inlet tube fitting.
    • Discard the O-ring seals.
  1. Disconnect the quick connect couplings and remove the crankcase ventilation tube. For additional information, refer to Section 310-00 .
  1. If equipped, disconnect the vacuum tube.
  1. Detach the engine harness retainer and position the harness aside.
  1. Remove the battery cover.
  1. Remove the transmission fluid level indicator tube retaining bolt and position the fluid indicator tube aside.

  1.  CAUTION: Do not use metal scrapers, wire brushes, power abrasive discs or other abrasive means to clean the sealing surfaces. These tools cause scratches and gouges which make leak paths. Use a plastic scraping tool to remove all traces of old sealant.

    NOTE: The bolts are part of the valve cover and should not be removed.

    Loosen the 14 fasteners and remove the RH valve cover and gasket.
    • Clean the valve cover mating surface of the cylinder head with silicone gasket remover and metal surface prep. Follow the directions on the packaging.
    • Discard the valve cover gasket. Clean the valve cover gasket groove with soap and water or a suitable solvent.

Installation

  1. NOTE: If the valve cover is not secured within 4 minutes, the sealant must be removed and the sealing area cleaned with silicone gasket remover and metal surface prep. Follow the directions on the packaging. Allow to dry until there is no sign of wetness, or 4 minutes, whichever is longer. Failure to follow this procedure can cause future oil leakage.

    Apply a bead of silicone gasket and sealant in 2 places where the engine front cover meets the cylinder head.
  1. Position the RH valve cover and new gasket on the cylinder head and tighten the 14 bolts in the sequence shown.
    • Tighten to 10 Nm (89 lb-in).
  1. Position the transmission fluid level indicator tube and install the bolt.
    • Tighten to 20 Nm (15 lb-ft).
  1. Install the battery cover.
  1. Position the engine harness and attach the retainer.
  1. If equipped, connect the vacuum tube.
  1. Position the crankcase ventilation tube and connect the quick connect couplings. For additional information, refer to Section 310-00 .
  1. Using new O-rings, connect the evaporator inlet tube fitting.
  1. Install the suction accumulator-to-A/C compressor manifold tube stud.
    • Tighten to 8 Nm (71 lb-in).
  1. Lubricate the new A/C compressor manifold tube O-ring seals with refrigerant compressor oil. Position the A/C compressor manifold tube and install the nut.
    • Tighten to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).
  1. Install the RH ignition coils. For additional information, refer to Section 303-07C .
  1. Install the RH VCT oil control solenoid. For additional information, refer to Section 303-14 .
  1. Install the PCM bracket and the PCM. For additional information, refer to Section 303-14 .
  1. Evacuate, leak test and charge the refrigerant system. For additional information, refer to Section 412-00 .
