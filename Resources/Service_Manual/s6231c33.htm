SECTION 303-01C: Engine — 5.4L (3V)
2006 F-150/Mark LT Workshop Manual
REMOVAL
Procedure revision date: 06/29/2005

Engine


Special Tool(s)
Modular Engine Lift Bracket
303-F047 (014-00073) or equivalent
Remover, Power Steering Pump Pulley
211-016 (T69L-10300-B)

All vehicles

  1. With the vehicle in NEUTRAL, position it on a hoist. For additional information, refer to Section 100-02 .
  1. Remove the hood.
  1. Remove the intake manifold. For additional information, refer to Intake Manifold in this section.
  1. Remove the accessory drive belt.
  1. Recover the A/C system. For additional information, refer to Section 412-00 .
  1. Remove the radiator. For additional information, refer to Section 303-03 .
  1. Remove the powertrain control module (PCM) and the support bracket. For additional information, refer to Section 303-14 .
  1. Remove the cowl panel grille. For additional information, refer to Section 501-02 .
  1. Detach the wiring harness and the windshield washer hose retainers from the cowl panel extension.
  1. Remove the bolts and the cowl extension panel.
  1. Disconnect the electrical connector and remove the bolt and the ground strap.
  1. Disconnect the heater hose.
  1. Remove the nut, disconnect the A/C manifold and tube assembly and position aside.
  1. Remove the nut and the A/C manifold and tube assembly and support bracket.
  1. Disconnect the coolant hose.
  1. Remove the bolt and position the power steering reservoir assembly aside.
  1. Disconnect the generator wiring harness retainer from the RH cylinder head.
  1. Disconnect the A/C compressor and the A/C high pressure cut-off switch electrical connectors.
  1. Disconnect the generator wiring harness retainer from the RH cylinder head and position the harness aside.
  1. Disconnect the crankshaft position (CKP) sensor wiring harness retainer from the starter motor wiring harness.
  1. Remove the nut and position aside the power steering pressure hose support bracket.
  1. Using the special tool, remove the power steering pump pulley.
  1. Disconnect the power steering pressure tube.
    • Drain the power steering fluid into a suitable container.
  1. Remove the 3 bolts and position the power steering pump aside.

Manual transmission vehicles

  1. Remove the clutch and pressure plate. For additional information, refer to Section 308-01 .

Automatic transmission vehicles

  1. Remove the nut and position aside the transmission cooler tube support bracket.
  1. Remove the starter. For additional information, refer to Section 303-06 .
  1. Remove the 2 bolts and the flexplate inspection cover.
  1. Remove the cylinder block opening cover.
  1. Remove the 4 torque converter-to-flexplate nuts.
    • Discard the nuts.
  1. Disconnect the shift cable and remove the shift cable bracket.

  1. NOTE: The upper 2 transmission-to-engine bolts will be removed later.

    Remove the lower 5 transmission-to-engine bolts.
  1. Remove the bolt and position the transmission fluid filler tube aside.

All vehicles

  1. Remove the drain plug and drain the engine oil.
  1. Disconnect the RH heated oxygen sensor (HO2S) electrical connector and detach the wiring harness retainer.
  1. Disconnect the LH heated exhaust gas oxygen sensor electrical connector and detach the electrical connector retainer.
  1. Remove the oil filter.
  1. Disconnect the engine oil temperature (EOT) sensor electrical connector.
  1. Remove the bolts and position the A/C compressor aside.
  1. If equipped, disconnect the block heater electrical connector.
  1. Detach the starter electrical harness support from the cylinder block.
  1. Remove the 4 exhaust manifold flange nuts.
  1. Remove the RH engine support insulator nuts and washer.
  1. Remove the LH engine support insulator bolt.

Automatic transmission vehicles

  1. Support the transmission.

  1. NOTE: On 4WD vehicles, it may be necessary to reposition the transfer case vent hose to access the bolts.

    Remove the upper 2 transmission-to-engine bolts.

All vehicles

  1. Install the special tool.
  1. Using a suitable floor crane, remove the engine assembly from the vehicle.
