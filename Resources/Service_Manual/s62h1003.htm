Principles of Operation

The headlamp system is a dual-beam pattern system. It consists of a low and high beam headlamp, with a replaceable bulb. The headlamp assembly has the park/turn indicator lamp integrated into the headlamp assembly. The headlamp switch routes voltage to the headlamps, the parking lamps and the autolamp system when demanded. All exterior lighting devices require between 11.3 and 14 volts for the lamps to illuminate correctly. An exterior lamp that appears to be dim may be caused by voltage that is below this level.

 

Inspection and Verification

  1. Verify the customer concern.
  1. Visually inspect the following for obvious signs of mechanical or electrical damage.

    Visual Inspection Chart
    Mechanical Electrical
    • Headlamp switch
    • Multifunction switch
    • Vehicle security module
    • Central junction box (CJB) fuse(s):
      • 23 (10A) (RH low beam)
      • 25 (10A) (LH low beam)
      • 35 (20A) (high beams)
      • 40 (20A) (headlamp switch)
    • High beam relay
    • Daytime running lamp (DRL) enable relay (if equipped)
    • Circuitry

  1. If an obvious cause for an observed or reported concern is found, correct the cause (if possible) before proceeding to the next step.
  1. If the concern is not visually evident, verify the symptom. GO to Symptom Chart .

Pinpoint Tests

 

Pinpoint Test A: Both Headlamps Are Inoperative

Normal Operation

Battery voltage is supplied to the headlamp switch through circuit 196 (DB/OG). When the headlamp switch is placed in the headlamps ON position, voltage is transferred to circuit 15 (RD/YE) which supplies voltage to the multifunction switch which then directs the voltage to the low beam headlamps or to the high beam relay.

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Circuit 15 (RD/YE) open
  • Circuit 196 (DB/OG) open
  • Headlamp switch
  • Multifunction switch
  • Central junction box (CJB)

Pinpoint Test B: The Low Beams Are Inoperative

Normal Operation

The multifunction switch receives voltage from the headlamp switch through circuit 15 (RD/YE). When the multifunction switch is in the LOW BEAM position, the voltage is sent through circuit 13 (RD/BK) to the central junction box (CJB) fuses 23 (10A) (RH headlamp) and 25 (10A) (LH headlamp). After passing through the fuses, voltage is then carried through circuits 161 (DG/OG) (RH headlamp) and 160 (DB/WH) (LH headlamp) to the low beam headlamps. Circuit 57 (BK) is the ground circuit for the headlamps.

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Circuit 13 (RD/BK) open
  • Multifunction switch
  • CJB

Pinpoint Test C: The High Beams Are Inoperative

Normal Operation

The multifunction switch receives voltage from the headlamp switch through circuit 15 (RD/YE). When the multifunction switch is placed in the HIGH BEAM position, voltage is routed to the high beam relay coil through circuit 275 (YE). When the high beam relay coil receives battery voltage from circuit 275 (YE), the high beam relay is energized. Battery voltage received from within the central junction box (CJB) is then routed to circuit 12 (LG/BK) via CJB fuse 35 (20A) and then to the high beam headlamps. Circuit 57 (BK) is the ground circuit for the headlamps and relay.

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Circuit 12 (LG/BK) open
  • Circuit 57 (BK) open
  • Circuit 275 (YE) open
  • High beam relay
  • Multifunction switch
  • CJB

Pinpoint Test D: One Low Beam Headlamp Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

The multifunction switch routes voltage through circuit 13 (RD/BK) to the central junction box (CJB) fuses 23 (10A) (RH headlamp) and 25 (10A) (LH headlamp). After passing through the fuses, voltage is then carried through circuits 161 (DG/OG) (RH headlamp) and 160 (DB/WH) (LH headlamp) to the low beam headlamps. Circuit 57 (BK) is the ground circuit for the headlamps.

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Circuit 13 (RD/BK) open
  • Circuit 57 (BK) open
  • Circuit 160 (DB/WH) (LH headlamp) open
  • Circuit 161 (DG/OG) (RH headlamp) open
  • CJB

Pinpoint Test E: The Headlamps Are On Continuously

Normal Operation

When the headlamp switch is placed in the HEADLAMPS ON position, voltage is routed to circuit 15 (RD/YE) which sends voltage to the multifunction switch. The vehicle security module also supplies voltage to circuit 15 (RD/YE) when the autolamp system has requested the exterior lamps on.

For low beam headlamps, the multifunction switch routes voltage through circuit 13 (RD/BK) to the central junction box (CJB) fuses 23 (10A) (RH headlamp) and 25 (10A) (LH headlamp). After passing through the fuses, voltage is then carried through circuits 161 (DG/OG) (RH headlamp) and 160 (DB/WH) (LH headlamp) to the low beam headlamps. If equipped with daytime running lamps (DRL), the DRL resistor also routes voltage to circuit 13 (RD/BK) (at a reduced voltage) when the ignition is in the RUN position and the high beams are off.

For high beam headlamps, the multifunction switch routes voltage to circuit 275 (YE) to the high beam relay. When the high beam relay receives battery voltage from circuit 275 (YE), the high beam relay is energized. Battery voltage received from the CJB is then routed to circuit 12 (LG/BK) via CJB fuse 40 (20A). Voltage is then carried to the high beam headlamps through circuit 12 (LG/BK). The fog lamp relay coil uses the high beam circuit as a ground when energized.

    Possible Causes
  • Circuit 12 (LG/BK) short to voltage
  • Circuit 13 (RD/BK) short to voltage
  • Circuit 15 (RD/YE) short to voltage
  • Circuit 160 (DB/WH) short to voltage
  • Circuit 161 (DG/OG) short to voltage
  • Circuit 275 (YE) short to voltage
  • High beam relay
  • Fog lamp relay
  • Headlamp switch
  • DRL system
  • CJB
  • Multifunction switch
  • Vehicle security module
