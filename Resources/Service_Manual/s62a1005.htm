SECTION 310-01: Fuel Tank and Lines
2006 F-150/Mark LT Workshop Manual
REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION
Procedure revision date: 09/22/2005

Fuel Tank

Removal and Installation

All vehicles

 WARNING: The evaporative emissions system contains fuel vapor and condensed fuel vapor. Although not present in large quantities, it still presents the danger of explosion or fire. Disconnect the battery ground cable from the battery to minimize the possibility of an electrical spark occurring, possibly causing a fire or explosion if fuel vapor or liquid fuel are present in the area. Failure to follow these instructions may result in personal injury.

 WARNING: Do not smoke or carry lighted tobacco or open flame of any type when working on or near any fuel-related component. Highly flammable mixtures are always present and can be ignited. Failure to follow these instructions may result in personal injury.

 WARNING: There are 2 fuel systems, an early and late build fuel system. The early build system uses a 7.9 mm (5/16 in) fuel line and connectors. The late build uses a 9.5 mm (3/8 in) fuel line and connectors. Do not interchange the 2 fuel line sizes or components because fuel leakage will take place causing a fire hazard. Failure to follow these instructions may result in personal injury.

  1. Drain the fuel tank. For additional information, refer to Section 310-00 .
  1. Disconnect the fuel filler pipe vent tube quick connect coupling. For additional information, refer to Section 310-00 .
  1. Disconnect the vapor and fuel tube quick connect couplings at the fuel tank. For additional information, refer to Section 310-00 .
  1. Disconnect the vapor and fuel tube quick connect couplings at the fuel filter. For additional information, refer to Section 310-00 .

Two-piece driveshaft with center bearing shim

  1. NOTE: Some 2-piece driveshafts will have a shim between the crossmember and the center bearing. If this shim is present then the following step must be carried out.

    Remove the driveshaft center bearing bolts and position and support the driveshaft aside.
    • To install, tighten to 48 Nm (35 lb-ft).

All vehicles

  1. Remove the 4 fuel tank skid plate nuts and the skid plate (if equipped).
    • To install, tighten to 20 Nm (15 lb-ft).
  1. Use a suitable jack to support the fuel tank.
  1. Remove the fuel tank support strap bolt-to-No. 4 crossmember and remove the fuel tank strap.
    • To install, tighten to 47 Nm (35 lb-ft).
  1. Remove the fuel tank support strap bolt-to-No. 3 crossmember and remove the fuel tank strap.
    • To install, tighten to 47 Nm (35 lb-ft).
  1. Using a suitable jack, lower the fuel tank down slightly to gain access to the electrical connectors.
  1. Disconnect the fuel vapor vent control tube assembly valve electrical connector.
  1. Disconnect the fuel pump module electrical connector.
  1. Using the suitable jack, lower the fuel tank.
  1. To install, reverse the removal procedure.
