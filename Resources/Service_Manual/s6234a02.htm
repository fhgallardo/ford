SECTION 303-04A: Fuel Charging and Controls — 4.2L
2006 F-150/Mark LT Workshop Manual
DESCRIPTION AND OPERATION
Procedure revision date: 06/29/2005

Fuel Charging and Controls

The fuel charging and controls system consists of the:

  • electronic throttle body (TB).
  • fuel rail.
  • fuel injectors.
  • fuel rail pressure sensor.
  • fuel pump driver module (FPDM).

The fuel charging and controls system is:

  • a sequential, multiport fuel injection (SFI) system.
  • pulse-width modulated.
  • mass air flow (MAF) controlled.

Electronic Throttle Body (TB)

 CAUTION: The throttle body must be removed from the vehicle to be cleaned. Do not hold the throttle plate open with any object that could scratch the bore or plate while servicing or cleaning the throttle body. For additional information, refer to Throttle Body in this section.

The electronic TB:

  • controls air supply to the upper intake manifold by electronically positioning the throttle plate.
  • is not adjustable.
  • cannot be cleaned.

Fuel Rail

The fuel rail:

  • delivers fuel to the individual fuel injectors.
  • receives fuel from the fuel supply line.

Fuel Injectors

The fuel injectors:

  • are electrically operated by the powertrain control module (PCM).
  • atomize the fuel as the fuel is delivered.
  • are deposit-resistant. Do not clean.
  • Each has an internal solenoid which opens a needle valve to inject fuel into the lower intake manifold.

Fuel Pressure Sensor

The fuel pressure sensor:

  • maintains constant pressure to the fuel injectors and throughout the entire system.
  • is spring loaded and operated by intake manifold vacuum.

The FPDM controls the fuel pump based on signals from the PCM.
