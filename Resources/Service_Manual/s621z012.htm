NOTE: Corrosion protection needs to be restored whenever it is necessary to sand or grind through painted surfaces or E-coat, or when bare metal repairs are made.

NOTE: Avoid applying corrosion protection to mechanical and electrical parts, electric motors, speakers, door locks and drain holes.

  1. The surfaces must be free of oil, dirt and other foreign material.
    1. Thoroughly clean and degrease metal surfaces using a good wax and grease remover.
    1. Adhesion will be improved by lightly abrading the surface.
    1. Apply anti-corrosion coating with a brush (natural hair brush only) or roller.
    1. The material may also be applied through spraying.
      • Shake the product until all particles are suspended.
      • Filter the material into a spray gun cup and dilute up to 10% with water.
      • Apply the material using a basecoat/clearcoat spray gun equipped with a 1.4 mm (0.05 in) tip.
      • Allow the material to flash dry to remove water and let air dry, or bake at 60°C (140°F) for 45 minutes.
    1. Apply at 2.5 MIL film thickness.
    1. Anti-corrosion products will reach full-bond strength within 48 hours at 21°C (70°F) and 60% relative humidity.

  1. NOTE: Make sure the corrosion protection material is fully cured before attempting to prime or paint the surface(s).

    When welding overlapping surfaces or substrates, apply corrosion protection between the surfaces prior to welding. When the surfaces have been welded, apply corrosion protection to the exterior surfaces or substrates.
  1. When treating exposed seams, apply the appropriate seam sealer, following product guidelines. Prime and paint as required.
