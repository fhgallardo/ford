Reversing Lamps

Refer to Wiring Diagrams Cell 93 for schematic and connector information.


Special Tool(s)
73III Automotive Meter
105-R0057 or equivalent
 

Principles of Operation

The reversing lamps receive voltage when the reversing lamp relay is energized. For vehicles equipped with an automatic transmission, the digital transmission range (TR) sensor routes voltage to the reversing lamp relay to energize the coil when the transmission is placed in REVERSE. For vehicles equipped with a manual transmission, the reversing lamp switch routes voltage to the reversing lamp relay to energize the coil when the transmission is placed in REVERSE.

  1. Verify the customer concern.
  1. Visually inspect the following for obvious signs of mechanical or electrical damage.

Pinpoint Tests

 

Pinpoint Test T: The Reversing Lamps Are Inoperative

Normal Operation

Switched battery voltage is normally provided to the digital transmission range (TR) sensor (automatic transmission) or to the reversing lamp switch (manual transmission) through circuit 295 (LB/PK). When the transmission is placed in REVERSE, the digital TR sensor or reversing lamp switch routes voltage to circuit 1043 (DG/YE) and circuit 140 (BK/PK), which provides voltage to the reversing lamps. Ground is provided to the reversing lamps through circuit 57 (BK).

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Circuit 140 (BK/PK) open
  • Circuit 295 (LB/PK) open
  • Circuit 1043 (DG/YE) open
  • Central junction box (CJB)
  • Digital TR sensor (automatic transmission)
  • Reversing lamp switch (manual transmission)
 
PINPOINT TEST T: THE REVERSING LAMPS ARE INOPERATIVE
Test Step Result / Action to Take
  T1 CHECK FOR VOLTAGE TO THE DIGITAL TR SENSOR OR THE REVERSING LAMP SWITCH  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Disconnect: Digital TR Sensor C167 or Reversing Lamp Switch C169 .
  • Key in ON position.
  • For vehicles equipped with an automatic transmission, measure the voltage between the digital TR sensor C167-9, circuit 295 (LB/PK), harness side and ground.

  • For vehicles equipped with a manual transmission, measure the voltage between the reversing lamp switch C169-1, circuit 295 (LB/PK), harness side and ground.

  • Is the voltage greater than 10 volts?
Yes
GO to T3 .

No
VERIFY the CJB fuse 14 (10A) is OK. If OK, GO to T2 .
  T2 CHECK CIRCUIT 295 (LB/PK) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Disconnect: CJB C270b.
  • Key in ON position.
  • Measure the voltage between the CJB C270b pin 1, circuit 295 (LB/PK), component side and ground.

  • Is the voltage greater than 10 volts?
Yes
REPAIR circuit 295 (LB/PK). TEST the system for normal operation.

No
REPAIR or INSTALL a new CJB. TEST the system for normal operation.
  T3 CHECK THE REVERSING LAMP OPERATION  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • For vehicles equipped with an automatic transmission, connect a fused (10A) jumper wire between the digital TR sensor C167-11, circuit 1043 (DG/YE), harness side and the digital TR sensor C167-9, circuit 295 (LB/PK), harness side.

  • For vehicles with a manual transmission, connect a fused (10A) jumper wire between the reversing lamp switch C169-2, circuit 1043 (DG/YE), harness side and the reversing lamp switch C169-1, circuit 295 (LB/PK), harness side.

  • Key in ON position.
  • Do the reversing lamps illuminate?
Yes
For vehicles equipped with an automatic transmission, INSTALL a new digital TR sensor. REFER to Section 307-01 . TEST the system for normal operation.

For vehicles equipped with a manual transmission, INSTALL a new reversing lamp switch. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
REMOVE the jumper wire. GO to T4 .
  T4 CHECK CIRCUITS 140 (BK/PK) AND 1043 (DG/YE) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Disconnect: LH Reversing Lamp C451 or RH Reversing Lamp C461.
  • For vehicles equipped with an automatic transmission, measure the resistance between digital TR sensor C167-11, circuit 1043 (DG/YE), harness side and LH reversing lamp C451-3 or RH reversing lamp C461-3, circuit 140 (BK/PK), harness side.

  • For vehicles equipped with a manual transmission, measure the resistance between the reversing lamp switch C169-2, circuit 1043 (DG/YE), harness side and the LH reversing lamp C451-3 or the RH reversing lamp C461-3, circuit 140 (BK/PK), harness side.

  • Is the resistance less than 5 ohms?
Yes
REPAIR circuit 57 (BK). TEST the system for normal operation.

No
REPAIR circuit 140 (BK/PK) or circuit 1043 (DG/YE) as necessary. TEST the system for normal operation.
 

Pinpoint Test U: The Reversing Lamps Are On Continuously

Normal Operation

Switched battery voltage is normally provided to the digital transmission range (TR) sensor (automatic transmission) or to the reversing lamp switch (manual transmission) through circuit 295 (LB/PK). When the transmission is placed in REVERSE, the digital TR sensor or reversing lamp switch routes voltage to circuit 1043 (DG/YE) and circuit 140 (BK/PK), which provides voltage to the reversing lamps. Ground is provided to the reversing lamps through circuit 57 (BK).

    Possible Causes
  • Circuit 140 (BK/PK) short to voltage
  • Circuit 1043 (DG/YE) short to voltage
  • Digital TR sensor (automatic transmission)
  • Reversing lamp switch (manual transmission)
  • Central junction box (CJB)
  • Parking aid module
  • Electrochromatic mirror
 
PINPOINT TEST U: THE REVERSING LAMPS ARE ON CONTINUOUSLY
Test Step Result / Action to Take
  U1 CHECK THE DIGITAL TR SENSOR OR REVERSING LAMP SWITCH  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Disconnect: Digital TR Sensor C167 or Reversing Lamp Switch C169.
  • Key in ON position.
  • Do the reversing lamps continue to illuminate?
Yes
GO to U2 .

No
For vehicles equipped with an automatic transmission, INSTALL a new digital TR sensor. REFER to Section 307-01 . TEST the system for normal operation.

For vehicles equipped with a manual transmission, INSTALL a new reversing lamp switch. TEST the system for normal operation.
  U2 CHECK THE CJB  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Disconnect: CJB C270b.
  • Key in ON position.
  • Do the reversing lamps continue to illuminate?
Yes
GO to U3 .

No
REPAIR or INSTALL a new CJB. TEST the system for normal operation.
  U3 CHECK THE PARKING AID MODULE  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Disconnect: Parking Aid Module C2023.
  • Key in ON position.
  • Do the reversing lamps continue to illuminate?
Yes
GO to U4 .

No
GO to U5 .
  U4 CHECK CIRCUIT 1043 (DG/YE) FOR A SHORT TO VOLTAGE  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Disconnect: Electrochromatic Mirror C911.
  • Key in ON position.
  • Do the reversing lamps continue to illuminate?
Yes
REPAIR the circuit. TEST the system for normal operation.

No
INSTALL a new inside mirror. TEST the system for normal operation.
  U5 CHECK FOR CORRECT PARKING AID MODULE OPERATION  
  • Disconnect all the parking aid module connectors.
  • Check for:
    • corrosion
    • pushed-out pins
  • Connect all the parking aid module connectors and make sure they seat correctly.
  • Operate the system and verify the concern is still present.
  • Is the concern still present?
Yes
INSTALL a new parking aid module. REFER to Section 413-13 . TEST the system for normal operation.

No
The system is operating correctly at this time. The concern may have been caused by a loose or corroded connector.
