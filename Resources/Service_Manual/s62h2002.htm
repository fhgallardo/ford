Principles of Operation

Vehicles equipped with remote keyless entry (RKE) have an illuminated entry feature, which enables the instrument cluster to illuminate the interior lamps (and if equipped, the puddle lamps in the exterior mirrors) when an unlock signal is received from the vehicle security module through the UBP network, or when any of the doors are opened. The instrument cluster controls the interior lamps illuminated entry feature by activating the battery saver relay. When active, the illuminated entry feature will be canceled when any of the following conditions are met:

  • 25 seconds have elapsed since the illuminated entry feature was activated and the interior lamp control is not activated (door ajar or dimmer control switch ON).
  • On vehicles equipped with RKE, the instrument cluster received a request from the vehicle security module (remote transmitter lock button pressed) and the interior lamp control is not activated.
  • The ignition is in the RUN or START position and the interior lamp control is not activated.

For vehicles equipped with RKE, the instrument cluster illuminates the interior lamps when any door is open. The interior lamps shut off when the doors are closed and the illuminated entry feature is not active or when the vehicle speed is greater than 15 km/h (6 mph). The interior lamps are not reactivated until the vehicle speed is less than 15 km/h (6 mph) and a new door open signal is received.

For vehicles without RKE, the instrument cluster illuminates the interior lamps and puddle lamps (if equipped) when any door is open. The instrument cluster will turn the interior lamps and puddle lamps (if equipped) OFF when the doors are closed.

Power to the on-demand lamps, illuminated vanity mirrors and map lamps are controlled by the battery saver relay.

Pinpoint Tests

 

Pinpoint Test A: The Interior Lamps Are Inoperative

Normal Operation

On vehicles equipped with remote keyless entry (RKE), the door ajar switches are hardwired to the vehicle security module. The vehicle security module monitors the status of all door ajar switches, and when a door is opened, transmits a dome lamp ON signal to the instrument cluster through UART based protocol (UBP) circuit 1906 (PK), indicating a door has been opened. When the instrument cluster receives the dome lamp ON signal from the vehicle security module, the instrument cluster energizes the interior lamp circuits, which illuminates the interior lamps and, if equipped, the puddle lamps in the exterior mirrors.

On vehicles without RKE, the door ajar switches are hardwired to the instrument cluster. When the driver or passenger door is opened, the instrument cluster receives a ground signal from the driver door ajar switch through circuit 344 (BK/YE), or from the passenger door ajar switch through circuit 345 (BK/PK), and the instrument cluster energizes the interior lamp circuits, causing the interior lamps to illuminate.

The dimmer control switch can be used to turn the interior lamps ON or OFF when the doors are closed. The interior lamp defeat switch can be used to turn the interior lamps OFF when the doors are open.

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Circuit 53 (BK/LB) open or short to ground
  • Circuit 57 (BK) open
  • Circuit 402 (OG/LG) open
  • Circuit 1405 (LB/BK) short to ground
  • Circuit 3049 (BK/LG) open
  • Dimmer control switch
  • Instrument cluster

Pinpoint Test B: An Individual Interior Lamp Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

The instrument cluster energizes circuit 53 (BK/LB) providing power to the interior lamps. The interior lamps are grounded through circuit 57 (BK). The interior lamps remain illuminated until the instrument cluster removes voltage from circuit 53 (BK/LB).

    Possible Causes
  • Circuit 53 (BK/LB) open
  • Circuit 57 (BK) open
  • Interior lamp

Pinpoint Test C: The Interior Lamps Stay On Continuously

Normal Operation

For vehicles equipped with RKE, the vehicle security module sends an interior lamps ON request to the instrument cluster through UART based protocol (UBP) network circuit 1906 (PK) whenever a door has been opened, the unlock button has been pressed on the remote transmitter, or when an unlock request has been entered with the keyless keypad (if equipped). The interior lamps can also be turned ON or OFF with the dimmer control switch, which provides a ground signal to the instrument cluster through circuit 402 (OG/LG) when the dimmer control switch is turned ON. When the instrument cluster receives the interior lamps ON request from the vehicle security module or the dimmer control switch, the instrument cluster energizes circuit 53 (BK/LB), which provides power to the interior lamps. The interior lamps are grounded through circuit 57 (BK). The interior lamps remain illuminated until the instrument cluster removes voltage from circuit 53 (BK/LB).

For non-RKE vehicles, the instrument cluster energizes circuit 53 (BK/LB) when a door has been opened, which provides power to the interior lamps. The interior lamps can also be turned ON or OFF with the dimmer control switch. The interior lamps are grounded through circuit 57 (BK), and will remain illuminated until the instrument cluster removes voltage from circuit 53 (BK/LB).

    Possible Causes
  • Circuit 53 (BK/LB) short to voltage
  • Circuit 402 (OG/LG) short to ground
  • Circuit 344 (BK/YE) short to ground
  • Circuit 345 (PK/BK) short to ground
  • Circuit 346 (BK/WH) short to ground
  • Circuit 363 (BK/LB) short to ground
  • Door ajar switch
  • Dimmer control switch
  • Instrument cluster

Pinpoint Test D: The Demand Lighting Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

The instrument cluster energizes the battery saver relay located in the central junction box (CJB) through circuit 1005 (VT/OG) when the ignition switch is in the START/RUN position. The battery saver relay provides power to circuit 705 (LG/OG). Circuit 705 (LG/OG) provides power to each demand lamp switch which when activated provides power to the selected demand lamp. The demand lamps are grounded through circuit 57 (BK). The battery saver deactivates the power circuit when the instrument cluster releases the ground from the relay on circuit 1005 (VT/OG) after time-out. The battery saver relay is integral to the CJB.

    Possible Causes
  • Fuse
  • Circuit 705 (LG/OG) open
  • Circuit 1005 (VT/OG) open or short to voltage
  • Instrument cluster
  • CJB

Pinpoint Test G: The Interior Lamps Do Not Turn On With One Door Open

Normal Operation

For vehicles equipped with remote keyless entry (RKE), the vehicle security module monitors the door ajar switch status on circuits 344 (BK/YE), 345 (BK/PK), 346 (BK/WH) and 363 (BK/LB) for a ground supplied through the ajar switches, by circuit 57 (BK) indicating a door has been opened. The vehicle security module transmits information over the network to indicate door ajar status. The instrument cluster then energizes the interior lamp circuits, lighting the interior lamps. On vehicles without RKE, the 2 front door ajar circuits 344 (BK/YE) and 345 (BK/PK) are monitored directly by the instrument cluster.

    Possible Causes
  • Circuit 57 (BK) open
  • Circuit 344 (BK/YE) open
  • Circuit 345 (BK/PK) open
  • Circuit 346 (BK/WH) open
  • Circuit 363 (BK/LB) open
  • Door ajar switch
  • Vehicle security module (if equipped)
  • Instrument cluster

Pinpoint Test H: An Individual Puddle Lamp Is Inoperative

Normal Operation

On vehicles equipped with remote keyless entry (RKE), when any door is opened, the vehicle security module sends an interior lamps ON signal to the instrument cluster through UART based protocol (UBP) circuit 1906 (PK). When the instrument cluster receives the interior lamps ON request from the vehicle security module, the instrument cluster will illuminate the puddle lamps by sending voltage to the puddle lamps through circuit 53 (BK/LB). Ground for the puddle lamps is provided through circuit 57 (BK).

On vehicles without RKE, the instrument cluster illuminates the puddle lamps when any door has been opened.

    Possible Causes
  • Circuit 53 (BK/LB) open
  • Circuit 57 (BK) open
  • Exterior mirror
