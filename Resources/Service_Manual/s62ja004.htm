Principles of Operation

The driver seat module (DSM) supports several vehicle subsystems and permits intermodule and diagnostic tool communications.

The memory driver seat feature allows the driver to program a personalized seat position that can be recalled using the memory switches. For additional information, refer to Section 501-10 .

The adjustable pedals are also controlled by the DSM. This system permits the adjustment of the brake and accelerator pedals using the adjustable pedal switch. For additional information, refer to Section 206-00 .

 

Inspection and Verification

  1. Verify the customer concern.
  1. Visually inspect for obvious signs of electrical damage.


Visual Inspection Chart
Electrical
  • Central junction box (CJB) fuse(s):
    • 3 (5A)
    • 109 (30A)
  • Circuitry
  • DSM

  1. If an obvious cause for an observed or reported concern is found, correct the cause (if possible) before proceeding to the next step.
  1. If the cause is not visually evident, connect the diagnostic tool to the data link connector (DLC) and select the vehicle to be tested from the diagnostic tool menu. If the diagnostic tool does not communicate with the vehicle:
  • check that the program card is correctly installed.
  • check the connections to the vehicle.
  • check the ignition switch position.
  1. If the diagnostic tool still does not communicate with the vehicle, refer to the diagnostic tool operating manual.
  1. Carry out the diagnostic tool data link test. If the diagnostic tool responds with:
  • CAN, ISO or UBP circuit fault; all electronic control units no response/not equipped, refer to Section 418-00 .
  • No response/not equipped for the DSM, Go To Pinpoint Test A .
  • System passed, retrieve and record the continuous diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs), erase the continuous DTCs, and carry out self-test diagnostics for the DSM.
  1. If the DTCs retrieved are related to the concern, go to the Diagnostic Trouble Code (DTC) Chart in this section.
  1. If no DTCs related to the concern are retrieved, GO to Symptom Chart .

Pinpoint Tests

 

Pinpoint Test A: No Communication With The Driver Seat Module (DSM)

Normal Operation

The DSM receives voltage from the central junction box (CJB) through circuits 566 (DG) and 956 (OG/LG). The DSM is grounded through circuits 875 (BK/LB) and 57 (BK).

    Possible Causes
  • fuse(s)
  • circuit 57 (BK) open
  • circuit 566 (DG) open
  • circuit 875 (BK/LB) open
  • circuit 956 (OG/LG) open
  • DSM
 
PINPOINT TEST A: NO COMMUNICATION WITH THE DRIVER SEAT MODULE (DSM)

 CAUTION: Use the correct probe adapter(s) when making measurements. Failure to use the correct probe adapter(s) may damage the connector.

Test Step Result / Action to Take
  A1 CHECK THE DSM C341b AND DSM C341c FOR DAMAGE  
  • Key in OFF position.
  • Disconnect: DSM C341b and C341c.
  • Inspect the DSM connectors for damage.
  • Are the DSM connectors OK?
Yes
GO to A2 .

No
REPAIR the DSM connector. CLEAR the diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs). REPEAT the self-test.
  A2 CHECK CIRCUITS 57 (BK) AND 875 (BK/LB) FOR AN OPEN  
  • Measure the resistance between the DSM C341c-6, circuit 875 (BK/LB), harness side and ground; and between the DSM C341b-2, circuit 57 (BK), harness side and ground.

  • Are the resistances less than 5 ohms?
Yes
GO to A3 .

No
REPAIR the circuit. CLEAR the DTCs. REPEAT the self-test.
  A3 CHECK CIRCUITS 566 (DG) AND 956 (OG/LG) FOR VOLTAGE  
  • Key in ON position.
  • Measure the voltage between the DSM C341c-1, circuit 956 (OG/LG), harness side and ground; and between DSM C341b-1, circuit 566 (DG), harness side and ground.

  • Are the voltages greater than 10 volts?
Yes
REFER to Section 418-00 to continue diagnosis of the communications network.

No
VERIFY the CJB fuses 3 (5A) and 109 (30A) are OK. If OK, REPAIR the circuit. CLEAR the DTCs. REPEAT the self-test.
