Removal and Installation

NOTE: If an evaporator core leak is suspected, the evaporator core must be vacuum leak tested before it is removed from the vehicle.

NOTE: Installation of a new suction accumulator is not required when repairing the air conditioning system, except when there is physical evidence of contamination from a failed A/C compressor or damage to the suction accumulator.

  1. Remove the heater core. For additional information, refer to Heater Core in this section.
  1. Remove the screws and detach the wire harness from the evaporator core cover.
  1. Remove the screws and the blower motor resistor (EMTC) or blower motor speed control (EATC).
  1. Remove the bottom evaporator core cover screws.
  1. Disconnect the air inlet mode door actuator electrical connector.
  1. Disconnect the air inlet mode door actuator electrical connector.
  1. Remove the remaining upper evaporator core covers screws and the evaporator core cover.
  1. Remove the evaporator core.
  1. To install, reverse the removal procedure.
    • Lubricate the refrigerant system with the correct amount of clean PAG oil. For additional information, refer to Section 412-00 .
