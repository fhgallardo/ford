SECTION 307-05: Automatic Transaxle/Transmission External Controls
2006 F-150/Mark LT Workshop Manual
REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION
Procedure revision date: 06/29/2005

Selector Lever Cable — Floor Shift

Removal

  1. With the vehicle in NEUTRAL, position it on a hoist. For additional information, refer to Section 100-02 .
  1. Remove the left console kick panel.
  1. Lift the console storage lid and remove the console finish panel.
  1. Disconnect the transmission shift cable from the transmission selector lever arm.
  1. Disconnect the transmission shift cable from the transmission selector bracket.
  1. Push the rubber grommet and transmission shift cable through the floor panel.
  1. Disconnect the shift cable from the transmission.
    1. Unlock the shift cable adjustment lock by rotating the lock upward and removing the cable from the manual lever.
    1. Disconnect the shift cable from the shift cable bracket.
  1. Remove the 2 bolts and the shift cable bracket.

Installation

  1. Position the shift cable bracket and install the 2 bolts.
    • Tighten to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).
  1. Push the rubber grommet and transmission shift cable through the floor panel.
  1. Connect the transmission shift cable to the transmission selector bracket.
  1. Connect the transmission shift cable to the transmission selector lever arm.
  1. Place the transmission range selector lever in the 1st position toward the rear of the vehicle.
  1. Move the transmission range selector forward 2 detents. This should be the (D) position.
  1. Rotate the manual lever forward until the travel stops. Then rotate the manual lever back 2 detents to the (D) position.
  1. Connect the shift cable to the shift cable bracket. Connect the shift cable to the manual control lever.
    1. Install the new shift cable retainer.
    1. Connect the shift cable to the shift cable bracket and rotate the lock downward to lock the adjuster.
  1. Verify that the engine will start in PARK and NEUTRAL. Verify that the reverse lamps will illuminate in the REVERSE position. If not, check the adjustment of the transmission range (TR) sensor. For additional information on adjusting the TR sensor, refer to Section 307-01 .
  1. Adjust the shift cable. For additional information, refer to Selector Lever Cable Adjustment in this section.
