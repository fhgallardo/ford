SECTION 211-02: Power Steering
2006 F-150/Mark LT Workshop Manual
REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION
Procedure revision date: 06/29/2005

Power Steering Pump

NOTE: 4.6L and 5.4L engine shown, 4.2L engine similar.


Item Part Number Description
1 W525923-S Suction line-to-power steering pump clamp
2 3A697 Suction line
3 3D763 Power steering pump pulley
4 — Pressure line fitting-to-pump (part of 3A719) (4.6L, 5.4L engines)
5 — Pressure line fitting-to-pump (part of 3A719) (4.2L engine)
6 W707225-S Power steering pump bolts (3 required)
7 W707225-S Power steering pump bolt (4.2L engine)
8 3A696 Power steering pump

Removal

All vehicles

  1. With the vehicle in NEUTRAL, position it on a hoist. For additional information, refer to Section 100-02 .
  1. Release the clamp and disconnect the suction line from the power steering pump.
    • Allow the fluid to drain into a suitable container.
  1. Remove the power steering pump pulley. For additional information, refer to Power Steering Pump Pulley in this section.
  1. Disconnect the pressure line fitting-to-pump and position the high pressure line aside.
    • Discard the Teflon® seal.
  1. Remove the power steering pump.

4.6L, 5.4L engines

  1. Remove the 3 power steering pump bolts.

4.2L engine

  1. Remove the 4 power steering pump bolts.

Installation

All vehicles

  1. Using the special tool, install a new Teflon® seal on the pressure line fitting.

4.6L, 5.4L engines

  1. Position the power steering pump and install the 3 bolts.
    • Tighten to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).

4.2L engine

  1. Position the power steering pump and install the 4 bolts.
    • Tighten to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).

All vehicles

  1. Position the high pressure line and connect the fitting to the power steering pump.
    • If equipped with a 4.6L or 5.4L engine, tighten to 65 Nm (48 lb-ft).
    • If equipped with a 4.2L engine, tighten to 20 Nm (15 lb-ft).
  1. Connect the suction line and install the clamp.
  1. Install the power steering pump pulley. For additional information, refer to Power Steering Pump Pulley in this section.
    • Fill the power steering system. For additional information, refer to Section 211-00 .
