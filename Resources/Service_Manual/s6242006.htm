SECTION 204-02: Rear Suspension
2006 F-150/Mark LT Workshop Manual
REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION
Procedure revision date: 03/10/2006

Spring

 WARNING: All vehicles are equipped with gas shock absorbers which will extend unassisted. Do apply heat or flame to the shock absorbers during removal or component servicing. Failure to follow these instructions may result in personal injury.

 CAUTION: Suspension fasteners are critical parts because they affect performance of vital parts and systems and their failure can result in major service expense. A new part with the same part number must be installed if installation becomes necessary. Do not use a replacement part of lesser quality or substitute design. Torque values must be used as specified during reassembly to make sure of correct retention of these parts.

All vehicles

  1. With the vehicle in NEUTRAL, position it on a hoist. For additional information, refer to Section 100-02 .
  1. Using a suitable jack, support the axle.
  1. Remove and discard the shock absorber lower nut and bolt.
    • To install, tighten to 90 Nm (66 lb-ft).

Light duty vehicles

  1. Remove the 4 U-bolt nuts, the U-bolt plate and the 2 U-bolts. Discard the nuts and the U-bolts.
    • To install, tighten the U-bolt nuts in 4 stages.
    • Stage 1: Tighten in a cross pattern to 35 Nm (26 lb-ft).
    • Stage 2: Tighten in a cross pattern to 70 Nm (52 lb-ft).
    • Stage 3: Tighten in a cross pattern to 100 Nm (74 lb-ft).
    • Stage 4: Tighten in a cross pattern to 133 Nm (98 lb-ft).

Heavy duty vehicles

  1. Remove the 4 U-bolt nuts, the U-bolt plate and the 2 U-bolts. Discard the nuts and the U-bolts.
    • To install, tighten the U-bolt nuts in 4 stages.
    • Stage 1: Tighten in a cross pattern to 63 Nm (46 lb-ft).
    • Stage 2: Tighten in a cross pattern to 125 Nm (92 lb-ft).
    • Stage 3: Tighten in a cross pattern to 188 Nm (139 lb-ft).
    • Stage 4: Tighten in a cross pattern to 250 Nm (184 lb-ft).

All vehicles

  1. NOTE: If removing the left rear spring, lower the fuel tank to gain access to the spring shackle-to-frame bolt. For additional information, refer to Section 310-00 .

    NOTE: If removing the right rear spring, remove the muffler to gain access to the spring-to-frame bolt. For additional information, refer to Section 309-00 .

    Remove and discard the spring-to-frame nut and bolt.
    • To install, tighten to:
      • 300 Nm (222 lb-ft) (vehicles built through 12/2005)
      • 350 Nm (258 lb-ft) (vehicles built after 12/2005)
  1. Remove and discard the spring shackle-to-frame nut and bolt.
    • To install, tighten to 133 Nm (98 lb-ft).

  1.  CAUTION: Only lower the axle enough to gain access to remove the spring.

    Lower the jack and remove the spring and shackle.
  1. If necessary, remove the spring-to-shackle nut, bolt and spring shackle.
    • Discard the nut and bolt.
    • To install, tighten to 133 Nm (98 lb-ft).

  1. NOTE: On 4x4 vehicles, make sure the spacer is correctly seated between the axle and spring stack, with the nose pointed inward.

    NOTE: The spring-to-frame nut and bolt orientation must be as shown in the exploded view.

    To install, reverse the removal procedure.
