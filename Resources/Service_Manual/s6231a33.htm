Removal

All vehicles

  1. With the vehicle in NEUTRAL, position it on a hoist. For additional information, refer to Section 100-02 .
  1. Release the fuel system pressure. For additional information, refer to Section 310-00 .
  1. Disconnect the battery ground cable. For additional information, refer to Section 414-01 .
  1. Drain the cooling system. For additional information, refer to Section 303-03 .
  1. Remove the hood.
  1. Remove the cowl panel grille. For additional information, refer to Section 501-02 .
  1. Remove the powertrain control module (PCM). For additional information, refer to Section 303-14 .
  1. Detach the electrical connector, the wiring harness retainer and the washer hose from the cowl extension panel.

Vehicles equipped with a manual transmission

  1. Remove the pushpins and position the clutch master cylinder reservoir aside. Disconnect the fuel vapor tubes from the purge valve.

All vehicles

  1. Remove the 7 bolts and the cowl extension panel.
  1. Remove the 9 pushpins and the radiator sight shield.
  1. Remove the upper intake manifold. For additional information, refer to Upper Intake Manifold in this section.
  1. Remove the radiator, the cooling fan and the shroud. For additional information, refer to Section 303-03 .
  1. Remove the air cleaner. For additional information, refer to Section 303-12 .
  1. Release the clamp and disconnect the upper radiator hose from the thermostat housing. Remove the hose from the vehicle.
  1. Release the clamps, disconnect the heater hoses and position the heater hoses aside.
  1. Release the clamp and disconnect the lower radiator hose from the coolant pump.
  1. Disconnect the intake manifold runner control (IMRC) actuator and the fuel charging wiring harness electrical connectors.
  1. Disconnect the fuel supply spring lock coupling. For additional information, refer to Section 310-00 .
  1. Remove the 4 bolts and the fuel rail, the fuel injectors, the fuel charging wiring harness and the vacuum harness as an assembly.

Vehicles equipped with an automatic transmission

  1. Disconnect the fuel vapor tubes from the purge valve.

All vehicles

  1. Using the special tool, with a twisting motion, pull the LH spark plug wires off the spark plugs.
  1. Detach the LH spark plug wire retainers and remove the LH spark plug wires.
  1. Remove the bolts and position the power steering fluid reservoir aside.
  1. If equipped, remove the compressor manifold and tube assembly. For additional information, refer to Section 412-01 .
  1. Rotate the accessory drive belt tensioner counterclockwise and remove the accessory drive belt.
  1. Remove the 2 power steering pump upper bolts.
  1. Disconnect the generator.
    • Disconnect the electrical connector.
    • Slide the protective cap up, remove the nut and disconnect the battery positive cable.
    • Detach the wiring harness retainer.
  1. Detach the wiring harness retainer.
  1. Using the special tool, with a twisting motion, pull the RH spark plug wires off the spark plugs.
  1. Detach the RH spark plug wire retainers.
  1. Disconnect the coil electrical connector.
  1. Remove the bolts, the coil and the RH spark plug wires as an assembly.
  1. Disconnect the positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) valve hose from the PCV valve and remove the hose.
  1. Disconnect the wiring harness in-line connector.
  1. Remove the bolt and detach the ground wire.
  1. Detach the wiring harness retainer from the rear of the RH cylinder head.

  1. NOTE: The front bolt cannot be removed from the power steering pump with the pressure hose in place. Remove the rear bolt first, then slide the power steering pump out while removing the second bolt.

    Position the power steering pump aside.
    1. Remove the bolt.
    1. Remove the bolt and position the power steering pump aside.
  1. Drain the engine oil. Install the drain plug when finished.
    • Tighten to 23 Nm (17 lb-ft).
  1. Remove the oil filter.

Vehicles equipped with an automatic transmission

  1. Detach the transmission cooler tubes from the bracket.

All vehicles

  1. Remove the nut and position the starter wiring harness aside.
  1. If equipped, disconnect the engine block heater electrical connector.
  1. Remove the four 3-way catalytic converter-to-exhaust manifold nuts.
  1. Remove the starter motor. For additional information, refer to Section 303-06 .

Vehicles equipped with an automatic transmission

  1. Remove the cylinder block opening cover.

  1. NOTE: Rotate the crankshaft using the crankshaft pulley bolt to access all of the torque converter nuts.

    NOTE: Mark the flexplate and one torque converter stud for installation reference.

    Remove the 4 torque converter nuts.

All vehicles

  1. Remove the 3 oil pan-to-transmission bolts.
  1. Remove the transmission-to-engine bolts.
  1. Remove the LH engine support insulator through bolt.
  1. Remove the RH engine support insulator nuts.
  1. Remove the stud bolt, the bolts and the power steering fluid reservoir bracket.
  1. Remove the nuts and the RH engine lifting eye.
  1. Install the special tool on the RH side of the engine.
  1. Remove the nuts and the LH engine lifting eye.
  1. Install the special tool on the LH side of the engine.

  1. NOTE: Use two 10 mm (3/8 in) spring links to connect the Engine Lifting Bracket 303-F047 to the Lifting Bracket, Engine 303-050.

    Install the special tool.
  1. Connect an engine crane to the special tool and lift the engine approximately 75 mm (3 in).
  1. Remove the bolts and the LH engine support insulator.
  1. Remove the bolts and the RH engine support insulator.
  1. Support the transmission with a floor jack and a wood block.

Vehicles equipped with an automatic transmission

  1. Remove the engine from the vehicle.

Vehicles equipped with a manual transmission

  1. Lift the engine while pulling the engine forward until the alternator pulley is above the radiator upper support.
  1. Rotate the engine 90 degrees to the left and remove the engine from the vehicle.
