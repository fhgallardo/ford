Principles of Operation

The parking aid system detects objects behind the vehicle when the vehicle is in REVERSE. The parking aid module does this by calculating the distance to an object by using 4 ultrasonic sensors mounted in the rear bumper. The ultrasonic field is emitted in a semicircular area around the rear of the vehicle. The sensors can detect objects approximately 1.8 m (5.9 ft) to the rear of the vehicle, or 50 cm (1.6 ft) to the rear side of the vehicle. A 750 kHz variable warning tone is generated from a speaker located on the parking aid module. The speaker increases its warning tone rate as the vehicle gets closer to an obstacle. When an object is detected within 25 cm (10 in) of the sensors, the warning tone becomes continuous.

NOTE: The tailgate must be closed for correct operation of the parking aid system.

The parking aid system is enabled when the ignition switch is in the RUN position and REVERSE is selected. The parking aid disabled indicator illuminates for 3 seconds when the ignition switch is turned to the RUN position. The system is disabled by pressing the parking aid switch on the instrument panel. The parking aid system indicates the system is disabled by illuminating the parking aid disabled indicator (in the parking aid switch). The system enables when the ignition is turned to the RUN position, or when the vehicle is put into REVERSE.

The parking aid system is disabled if a fault is detected in 1 of the 4 sensors, the parking aid speaker or the parking aid module. This is indicated by the parking aid disabled indicator illuminating when the vehicle is put into REVERSE.

Pinpoint Tests

 

Pinpoint Test A: No Communication With The Parking Aid Module

Normal Operation

Voltage is supplied to the parking aid module on circuit 294 (WH/LB). The parking aid module is grounded through circuit 875 (BK/LB). The parking aid module communicates with the diagnostic tool through the international standards organization (ISO) communications network.

    Possible Causes
  • fuse
  • parking aid module
  • circuit 294 (WH/LB) open
  • circuit 875 (BK/LB) open

Pinpoint Test B: The Parking Aid Is Inoperative / Does Not Operate Correctly

Normal Operation

The parking aid system is enabled when the ignition switch is in the RUN position and REVERSE gear is selected. When REVERSE is selected, voltage is supplied from the reversing lamps to the parking aid module through circuit 1043 (DG/YE). The parking aid module supplies voltage to the parking aid sensors on circuit 1439 (BN/PK). The parking aid module supplies ground to the parking aid sensors on circuit 1440 (DG/YE). The parking aid module receives a signal from the parking aid sensors on circuits 1444 (DB/YE), 1443 (WH/LB), 1442 (DG/WH) and 1441 (WH/LG). The system can be disabled through the parking aid switch. A 750 Hz variable warning tone is generated from a parking aid speaker. The parking aid speaker increases the warning tone rate as the vehicle gets closer to an obstacle.

    Possible Causes
  • circuit 1043 (DG/YE) open or short to ground
  • circuit 1435 (DG/VT) open or short to ground
  • circuit 57 (BK) open
  • parking aid sensor
  • parking aid module
  • parking aid switch
  • rear bumper

Pinpoint Test E: DTCs C1701, C1704, C1707 and C1710 Sensor Circuit Fault

Normal Operation

The parking aid system is enabled when the ignition switch is in the RUN position and REVERSE gear is selected. The parking aid module calculates the distance to an object by the use of 4 ultrasonic sensors mounted in the rear bumper. The parking aid sensors can detect obstructions in the path behind the vehicle. A 750 Hz variable warning tone is generated from a parking aid speaker. The parking aid speaker increases the warning tone rate as the vehicle gets closer to an obstacle.

If no objects are present, the parking aid PIDs should read 255 cm (100 in).

    Possible Causes
  • dirty or obstructed parking aid sensor
  • parking aid sensor
  • parking aid bumper harness
  • parking aid module

Normal Operation

The system can be disabled by the parking aid switch. A 750 Hz variable warning tone is generated from a parking aid speaker. The parking aid speaker increases the warning tone rate as the vehicle gets closer to an obstacle.
